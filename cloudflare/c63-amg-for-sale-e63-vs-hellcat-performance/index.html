Quick take: is an E63 faster than a Hellcat?

The short answer is: in stock form an AMG E63 S typically has the edge in 0-60 launches because of AMG 4MATIC+ all wheel drive and launch systems, while Hellcat variants often match or beat the E63 in quarter mile ETs when their higher peak power is the deciding factor. Independent tests and manufacturer specifications show the E63 S posting roughly 0-60 times near 3.0 seconds and quarter mile runs close to 11.1 seconds, while Hellcat models usually run mid-3 to low-4 second 0-60s and quarter mile ETs in the 11.0 to 11.5 second range depending on trim and conditions Car and Driver E63 test.

How the two cars differ: engine, drivetrain and factory power figures

Factory Mercedes AMG E63 S models come with a twin turbo V8 paired with AMG 4MATIC+ all wheel drive and factory power figures that commonly sit in the high-500s to low-600s horsepower range depending on model year and exact trim, which shapes the E63 S performance baseline Mercedes AMG technical specifications.

Dodge SRT Hellcat models use a supercharged HEMI V8 and factory ratings are often higher in raw peak horsepower, particularly for Redeye or equivalent high-output variants, where factory figures enter the 700+ horsepower band; that higher peak power is a key reason Hellcats can post very competitive quarter mile ETs Dodge Hellcat specifications.

Drivetrain layout and supporting electronics matter beyond peak power. AMG electronics, AWD hardware and adaptive transmissions add complexity and weight compared with the simpler RWD Hellcat layout, and published curb weights for both platforms commonly fall in a similar broad range depending on options and body style. That mix of mass, power and traction is central to how the cars behave off the line and down the quarter mile Mercedes AMG technical specifications.

Performance numbers: 0-60 and quarter mile comparison and note for c63 amg for sale shoppers

Measured runs from authoritative magazines place the E63 S near 0-60 in about 3.0 seconds and quarter mile ETs close to 11.1 seconds in well-executed tests; Hellcat variants in factory form typically show 0-60 times in the mid-3 to low-4 second band with quarter mile ETs that commonly sit between roughly 11.0 and 11.5 seconds depending on the specific Hellcat variant and test conditions Car and Driver E63 test.

The E63 S advantage in 0-60 is largely traction driven, thanks to AWD and AMG launch systems, while Hellcats can capitalize on higher peak horsepower to equal or exceed E63 quarter mile ETs when traction and gearing line up or when a Hellcat is a higher output Redeye variant Car and Driver Hellcat Redeye test.

Why drivetrain and traction change launch performance

AWD improves initial traction by sending torque to multiple wheels, which reduces wheelspin and helps a high-power car convert engine output into forward acceleration more effectively during a standing start. That is a practical reason E63 S models with AMG 4MATIC+ often record quicker 0-60 times than similarly powered RWD cars under comparable conditions Mercedes AMG technical specifications.

Sophisticated traction control and launch systems tune engine output, transmission behavior and torque distribution to optimize the initial launch. Those electronic strategies can shave tenths of a second off 0-60 times when they operate on a suitable surface and with appropriate tires Car and Driver E63 test.

Real world advantage depends on tire quality, surface conditions and driver technique. On loose or cold pavement, an AWD car typically retains a clear edge. On a well prepared drag surface with sticky tires, a RWD Hellcat can still match or beat an AWD rival if traction, gearing and power delivery are optimized Car and Driver Hellcat Redeye test.

Tuning and the aftermarket: when a Hellcat can outpace an E63

The Hellcat platform has a large aftermarket and is frequently modified with upgrades that raise peak power well beyond factory ratings, so a tuned Hellcat can and often does outpace a stock E63 S in straight line runs when modifications are properly executed Car and Driver Hellcat Redeye test.

Common upgrades that change ETs include supercharger upgrades, engine calibration or tunes, strengthened driveline components, and larger or stickier tires. Each modification affects durability, inspection needs and resale considerations in different ways, and they can materially increase insurance and maintenance costs for the buyer Dodge Hellcat specifications.

Weight, gearing and tires: why numbers vary between tests

Mass matters: AWD systems, extra electronics and optional equipment increase curb weight and inertia, which can blunt acceleration even when the engine makes strong power. Published curb weights for both the AMG and Hellcat families commonly fall in a broad similar range, so weight differences are one of several compensating factors when comparing times Mercedes AMG technical specifications.

Gear ratios and transmission programming change how power reaches the wheels across the acceleration run. Two cars with similar peak horsepower can post different ETs because one holds a higher ratio in first or has quicker shift logic, which affects the shape of the acceleration curve and the ultimate quarter mile ET MotorTrend comparison.

Tires and contact patch are often the limiting factor at launch. Test cars in magazines are sometimes equipped with semi-sticky or recommended test tires that improve launches; street tires on a used listing will usually give slower real world times, so compare the tire specification used in test numbers against the tires on the listed vehicle Car and Driver E63 test.

How to read magazine tests and what they mean for real world driving

Magazine test numbers are useful for relative comparison but reflect specific conditions: the test surface, ambient temperature, altitude, driver skill and the exact tires and fuel used all shape the results. Treat published ETs as comparatives rather than guarantees for any single used listing Car and Driver E63 test.

How to weigh ownership costs: insurance, fuel and maintenance

Insurance premiums for high performance models are typically higher than average and are sensitive to model, year, driver profile and any recorded modifications. Insurers often charge more for cars with higher factory power or documented aftermarket power increases, so get local quotes for the specific vehicle rather than relying on a general rule of thumb Edmunds used performance car guide.

Fuel costs for V8 performance cars tend to be higher in typical use than for smaller engines. Actual consumption depends heavily on driving style, local fuel prices and whether the car is largely used for short trips or steady highway driving Edmunds used performance car guide.

Estimate total cost to own by combining local insurance quotes, expected fuel usage for the powertrain, and likely maintenance needs. A well documented service history and a history report that shows regular maintenance can reduce uncertainty about upcoming repairs and help you budget more reliably.

Decision framework: when an E63 makes more sense and when a Hellcat does

If your priorities are traction in varied weather, refined electronics and quicker everyday acceleration in many tests, an E63 S often fits those needs because AWD and AMG systems help in daily driving and wet or cold conditions Mercedes AMG technical specifications.

If you prioritize peak straight line power per dollar, a large aftermarket for tuning, and the option to accept a simpler RWD setup for drag or track work, a Hellcat platform may be a better fit. Be explicit about the trade offs: tuning potential increases performance but also increases inspection needs and insurance costs Dodge Hellcat specifications.

Nonperformance factors to weigh include service availability, dealer familiarity with the platform, expected repair costs and how modifications will affect resale. Use those points as a tie breaker when two listings show similar documented performance.

Common mistakes buyers make with performance cars

Buyers commonly overemphasize peak horsepower while overlooking drivetrain, gearing and traction, which together determine the real world acceleration that matters to the buyer. Verify whether the published power figure is measured at the crank or wheel and whether drivetrain losses or gearing differences were addressed in the comparison Mercedes AMG technical specifications.

Another frequent error is accepting a modified car without complete documentation. For modified Hellcats especially, ask for dyno sheets, installation invoices and evidence that driveline and transmission components were upgraded if power has been increased significantly Edmunds used performance car guide.
