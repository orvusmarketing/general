Safety and tech: how to verify crash ratings and driver aids by model and trim

Safety performance varies across model years and trims. Do not assume a brand-level safety advantage; instead confirm IIHS and NHTSA crash ratings and the availability of active-safety packages for the exact vehicle you are considering Consumer Reports brand reliability

Features to verify on the listing and in the vehicle history report: forward collision mitigation, blind-spot monitoring, lane-keeping assist and whether optional packages listed by the dealer match the vehicle's equipment. If crash test results differ by year, prefer the year and trim with the higher rating.

Check feature fitment carefully: an identical model name can have different standard safety equipment in different trims or markets, and package options sometimes change between adjacent model years. Confirm feature presence before relying on a dealer description.

Common mistakes and pitfalls when choosing between BMW and Audi

Frequent errors include assuming brand equals reliability, skipping model-year checks, and relying on a single listing without cross-checking history reports. These mistakes can lead to unexpected repair costs or mismatched expectations at the time of purchase Edmunds car buying guide

Consequences and simple fixes:

  • Error: assuming a model is reliable because it carries a brand name. Fix: check dependability studies for the specific model year and order the vehicle history report.
  • Error: ignoring dealer reviews or financing terms. Fix: research dealer transparency signals and ask for written certification details.

Always book a pre-purchase inspection when ownership history or service records are incomplete, and verify title status and mileage discrepancies before you sign. Treat any major gaps in documentation as negotiation points or reasons to walk away.
