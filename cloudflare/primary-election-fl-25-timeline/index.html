What the primary election is and why it matters for FL-25

A primary election is the process states use to select party nominees for the general election, and in Florida the Division of Elections publishes the official schedule and explanation of those events, including statutory deadlines and election activities Elections Dates & Activities Calendar. The phrase primary election refers to the contest where voters affiliated with a party and eligible voters choose which candidate will appear as that party's nominee on the November ballot. See the National Conference of State Legislatures for a summary of state primary dates 2026 State Primary Election Dates.

For voters in Florida's 25th Congressional District, the primary determines which party nominees may appear on the general election ballot in November. That listing can help voters see who has declared and how the primary field is shaping up.

Where to find the official 2026 primary election dates - Division of Elections calendar

The authoritative statewide source for primary and general election dates in Florida is the Elections Dates & Activities Calendar published by the Division of Elections. That calendar lists scheduled dates for the primary and the general election, and it is the place to look for the official timeline that state law references Elections Dates & Activities Calendar. Use the calendar entry that has the most recent version date shown on the page. The published calendar binder is also available as a PDF Election Dates and Activities Calendar PDF.

The calendar is organized around statutory milestones and administrative activities. Typical items you will see listed include the date of the statewide primary, the date of the general election, candidate filing deadlines, and voter registration cutoffs. Those entries reflect statutory deadlines, which is why any numeric date should be validated against the calendar entry and the law it references.

When you look at the calendar, check the page for a version or publication date and for notes about changes. If a later version is posted, rely on the most recent calendar update. How to verify candidates and campaign finance records for the FL-25 primary

To confirm who is listed for the primary ballot in FL-25, begin with an updated candidate listing and district overview maintained by a public political reference that tracks declared candidates and election filings for the 2026 cycle Florida's 25th Congressional District. That listing helps you see which candidates have publicly declared and which offices are contested in the primary.

For primary-source verification of filings and committee names, use the Federal Election Commission search tools to look up candidate committees and campaign finance reports. The FEC database provides the official records for candidate and committee filings, disclosure reports, and fundraising totals filed under committee names Candidates, Committees and Campaign Finance. When reporting or checking fundraising or committee details, cite the FEC filing as the primary source for the record.

When you check candidate listings and finance records, note the name of the campaign committee exactly as it appears on filings and use that name when searching the FEC site. That helps avoid misattributing reports or mixing records from similarly named committees.

Common mistakes and pitfalls when planning for the FL-25 primary

A frequent mistake is relying on secondary summaries or cached pages that do not reflect the most recent Division of Elections calendar.
