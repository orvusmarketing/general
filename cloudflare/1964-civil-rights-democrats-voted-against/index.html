Quick answer: How many Democrats voted against the Civil Rights Act of 1964?

Short answer: In the U.S. Senate final passage on June 19, 1964 the Civil Rights Act passed 73 627, with 21 of the 27 recorded "nay" votes cast by Democrats, and in the U.S. House on July 2, 1964 the measure passed 289 6126 with the 126 nays predominantly cast by Democrats from the South. Common misunderstandings when counting 'Democrats who opposed the act'

One frequent confusion mixes party labels with region: while most nays were Democrats, many Democrats voted in favor; the Senate roll-call shows that Democratic opposition was concentrated but not universal, so counting "Democrats who opposed the act" requires checking the member-level lists rather than assuming all Democrats were opposed Senate roll-call votes 6 final passage.

Another common error is to conflate cloture and final passage; the cloture vote that ended the filibuster was a separate roll-call that won bipartisan backing and it enabled the later final passage vote, so readers should treat the cloture result and the final tally as distinct entries in the record Civil Rights Act of 1964 6 Encyclopedia entry.
