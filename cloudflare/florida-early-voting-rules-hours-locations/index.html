According to Florida Statutes, the state defines the early voting period and sets the legal framework that allows early voting, while leaving operational details to county supervisors. Who sets early voting sites and hours in Florida

Role of county supervisors of elections

State law delegates authority to county supervisors of elections to establish specific early voting sites and the hours each site will operate within the state’s early voting window, so supervisors make local schedule decisions for their county. That delegation explains why hours and site counts vary from county to county. Florida Statutes Section 101.657

How counties decide hours and locations

Factors county offices typically consider

Counties typically weigh local population size, where voters live, historical turnout patterns, and the availability of trained staff when choosing how many early voting sites to open and what hours to offer. How operational capacity affects site counts

Administrative capacity, including the number of election workers and available budget for leased sites, shapes whether a county opens more locations with shorter hours or fewer locations with longer hours. Counties balance access against the resources they can deploy. Florida Statutes Section 101.657

Why early voting hours differ by county

Not a difference in law, but in implementation

The difference in early voting hours across Florida counties is not a difference in legal rules; rather, state law provides a common framework and supervisors implement schedules that fit local conditions. That means variation is expected and lawful when supervisors publish their plans within the state window. Florida Statutes Section 101.657

Florida law sets the early voting period while county supervisors of elections choose specific sites and hours within that window, and the Division of Elections publishes the statewide calendar that lists the early voting window.

Typical tradeoffs counties make

Counties commonly trade off longer daily hours at fewer sites against more sites that each operate for shorter periods; that choice depends on local turnout history and logistical capacity. Practical tradeoffs also include staff availability, facility rental costs, and proximity to public transit. National Conference of State Legislatures overview

Where you can vote during early voting and voter rights

Voting at any designated site within your county

A registered voter may cast an early ballot at any designated early voting site within the county of registration during the hours the county has published; your eligibility to use a site is tied to your county, not to the particular site address. The county page lists official site names and addresses. Florida Division of Elections early voting page

What to check before you go

Before you leave to vote, confirm the site address and published hours on your county supervisor's page and verify the site appearance and directions if you rely on a mapping app. If you need special accommodations, contact the supervisor's office ahead of time. Florida Statutes Section 101.657
