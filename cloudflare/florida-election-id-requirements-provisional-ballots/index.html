Casting a provisional ballot preserves the voter record and creates a path for verification, rather than preventing a person from participating on Election Day. What is a provisional ballot in Florida?

Definition and legal purpose

A provisional ballot is a temporary ballot used when a voter's eligibility or identity cannot be immediately confirmed at the polling place; the ballot records choices while officials verify whether the voter meets requirements to have the ballot counted. The state guidance describes provisional ballots as a measure that protects voting rights pending verification Florida Division of Elections provisional ballots page.

When provisional ballots are used at the polling place

Typical reasons a provisional ballot is offered include lack of an acceptable ID, a name that does not match the precinct roster, or voting at the wrong precinct. Election officials use the provisional process so voters can make their choices on Election Day while officials resolve verification questions after the fact, consistent with federal best practices on handling provisional ballots EAC provisional ballots guidance.

Who can cast a provisional ballot and when to expect it

Eligible voters and common polling scenarios

Voters who cannot show an acceptable form of ID at the polling place or whose eligibility cannot be immediately confirmed may be offered a provisional ballot so the county can verify qualifications later. State guidance outlines that provisional ballots are available when ID or other verification is missing at check-in Florida Division of Elections provisional ballots page.

What poll workers must tell you at the time

Poll workers should explain that the vote will be recorded as provisional, give the voter instructions on next steps and the deadline for submitting ID or documents, and provide contact information for the county Supervisor of Elections. How to submit ID or documentation after casting a provisional ballot

Standard verification steps

After casting a provisional ballot, most voters must provide an acceptable form of ID or other documentation to the county Supervisor of Elections by the deadline set in state guidance or county rules; officials then review submitted materials and voter records to decide whether to count the ballot Florida Division of Elections provisional ballots page.

Accepted submission methods and what varies by county

Counties typically describe accepted methods for submitting ID, which can include in-person delivery at the Supervisors office, mail, or other channels allowed by the county. Exact methods, hours, and delivery rules differ across counties, so confirm the accepted submission methods on your local Supervisor of Elections website Miami-Dade County provisional ballot information.

Missing the submission deadline

Submitting the wrong type of ID or incomplete documentation

Practical scenarios and examples

Lost wallet on Election Day

If a voter loses their wallet and lacks an acceptable ID, they can cast a provisional ballot at the polling place and then provide a qualifying document to the Supervisor of Elections by the stated deadline. This preserves the voters ability to make choices on Election Day while giving time to complete verification Florida Division of Elections provisional ballots page.

Student using a campus ID

Some student or campus IDs may be acceptable if the state list explicitly includes them; before relying on a campus ID, check the states current acceptable ID list so you know whether that document will satisfy verification Florida Division of Elections voter ID information.

Voter at the wrong precinct

A voter who casts a provisional ballot because they are at the wrong precinct should follow the same ID submission and verification steps as other provisional voters; county verification still applies and the voter should use the Supervisor of Elections contact information to track status and deadlines Miami-Dade County provisional ballot information.

What to do if your provisional ballot is rejected

Where to find the rejection reason

If a provisional ballot is not counted, the county typically posts the determination and a reason; check the county status page or contact the Supervisor of Elections to learn why a ballot was rejected and to request any available documentation on the decision Florida Division of Elections provisional ballots page.

Possible next steps and resources

There is no automatic re-submission for the same election if a provisional ballot is rejected; questions about determinations or further remedies should be directed to the Supervisor of Elections in the county where you cast the ballot, and you can consult the state pages for official guidance Florida Division of Elections provisional ballots page.
