What an FEC filing is and why it matters

Definition and legal role, FEC filing

At its core, an FEC filing is the official disclosure a federal campaign committee submits that documents receipts, disbursements, loans and debts for a reporting period. The FEC filing help pages describe the purpose of these reports and where to find the official files for each committee FEC filing help pages.

Who files and what types of reports exist

Registered candidate committees, party committees and other covered committees file periodic reports that aggregate schedule data into summary totals. Common periodic forms include Form 3 and Form 3X, which collect schedule entries and present committee-level subtotals and totals; the Form 3X instructions explain how those totals must be prepared Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

How schedules map to a filing: an overview

What schedules do in a report

Schedules are the line level detail of a report; they list each receipt, disbursement, loan or debt entry that the committee must report. These line items feed specific subtotals on the periodic report, and the form instructions describe which schedule fields are required and when itemization is necessary Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Each schedule has required fields and itemization thresholds that determine whether a transaction appears as an individual line or within an aggregate. The guidance for Schedules A and B explains the contributor and payee fields committees must provide when itemization thresholds are met Schedules A and B: Itemized Receipts and Disbursements (FEC Guidance).

How periodic totals are calculated and where cash on hand appears

How aggregates are computed

Aggregates such as total receipts and total disbursements are calculated from the sum of schedule entries and committee subtotals according to rules in the form instructions; those instructions show which schedule subtotals roll into each line on Form 3 and Form 3X Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Schedules A through D are the item-level records that roll into the subtotals and summary lines on Form 3 and Form 3X; reading schedule subtotals and comparing them to the form summary lines is the standard way to verify totals against the committee PDF.

Common line items that determine cash on hand

Cash on hand is typically derived from totals for beginning cash, receipts, disbursements, loans and adjustments; the form instructions and FEC guidance describe the specific lines and arithmetic used to report cash on hand on periodic reports FEC filing help pages.

How schedules map to a filing: an overview

What schedules do in a report

Schedules function as the unit records that explain how a committee arrived at its summary totals. When you open a report, think of schedules as the ledger entries that add up to the headline numbers you see on Form 3 or Form 3X. The form instructions clarify the required fields and how schedule subtotals feed totals Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Schedule A: itemized receipts and what to look for

Fields on Schedule A

Schedule A lists itemized receipts and typically shows contributor name, contributor address, occupation, employer, date of receipt and contribution amount when the contribution meets the itemization rules. The FEC guidance for Schedule A lists these required fields and explains the conditions that trigger itemization Schedules A and B: Itemized Receipts and Disbursements (FEC Guidance).

When a contribution must be itemized

A contribution is itemized on Schedule A when it meets or exceeds the threshold described in the form instructions or when other reporting conditions apply. The instructions show the threshold levels and any special cases to watch for, so always check the current Form 3X instructions before drawing firm conclusions from a partial dataset Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Schedule B: itemized disbursements and common entries

Fields on Schedule B

Schedule B reports itemized disbursements and generally includes the payee name, amount, date and purpose or description of the expenditure when itemization rules apply. The Schedule B guidance describes which fields a committee must include for each reported disbursement Schedules A and B: Itemized Receipts and Disbursements (FEC Guidance).

Typical categories of disbursements

Common disbursement categories you will see are vendor payments for advertising, staff payroll or consultant fees, event expenses, travel and transfers to other committees. The form instructions help you distinguish a single large vendor payment from multiple itemized entries that reflect many small transactions Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Schedules C and D: loans, debts and offsets explained

Schedule C for loans

Schedule C is the place committees report loans made to the committee or loans the committee has made. Required loan fields and reporting rules for loans are set out in the FEC guidance so readers can see principal, interest and repayment status where applicable Schedules C and D: Loans, Debts and Offsets (FEC Guidance).

Schedule D for debts and offsets

Schedule D captures outstanding debts, offsets and adjustments that affect committee obligations. These entries explain liabilities that may not be apparent from receipts and disbursements alone and they feed subtotals used to compute net cash position on the periodic report Schedules C and D: Loans, Debts and Offsets (FEC Guidance).

How periodic totals are calculated and where cash on hand appears

How aggregates are computed

Report-level aggregates such as total receipts, total disbursements, and cash on hand are computed by summing schedule subtotals and applying the calculation rules in the form instructions; those instructions show how to reconcile schedule line sums with the Form 3 or Form 3X summary lines Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Common line items that determine cash on hand

Key lines to check when verifying cash on hand include beginning cash balance, receipts, disbursements, loans received or repaid and any adjustments for offsets; these items are explicitly tied to schedule subtotals in the instructions and should reconcile when the schedules are read correctly FEC filing help pages.

Where to find official filings and reliable third-party copies

Using FEC.gov search and bulk data

All filed reports and supporting schedules are publicly accessible on FEC.gov through the reports search and bulk data services; for authoritative figures you should retrieve the committee PDF or official data file on FEC.gov and cite that copy FEC filing help pages.

Third-party databases and when to cite FEC copies

Third-party sites like OpenSecrets and ProPublica republish FEC data and offer search tools that can be faster for discovery, but those copies are not a substitute for the official FEC PDF when you need to cite exact totals or certified records How to Read FEC Filings and Use Campaign Finance Data. Step-by-step method: how to read a specific filing

Step 1 identify committee and report

Start by noting the committee name and committee ID, and confirm the report period. Those metadata items typically appear at the top of the PDF and let you match schedules to the correct reporting window FEC filing help pages.

Step 2 open the PDF or data file

Download the report PDF or the structured data file from FEC.gov. Open the schedule sections in order and note subtotals after each schedule so you can compare them later to the report summary lines Understanding FEC Data and Searching Filings (ProPublica guide).

Step 3 check schedules against aggregates

Compare the subtotal at the end of Schedule A to the form line for total receipts, then repeat for Schedule B and total disbursements. For loans and debts, verify Schedule C and Schedule D subtotals against the lines for loans and obligations on the Form 3 or Form 3X Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Common mistakes and pitfalls when interpreting filings

Misreading aggregates

A frequent error is assuming a single schedule column equals a final total without checking the subtotals and any adjustments. Rounding, partial exports and omitted schedules can produce apparent mismatches unless you consult the FEC PDF and the instructions that explain aggregation rules Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Confusing third-party republished data with official copies

Another common pitfall is treating third-party search results as definitive. Third-party tools are useful for discovery, but when you quote totals or report dates, cite the FEC PDF so readers can see the original filing and context How to Read FEC Filings and Use Campaign Finance Data. Practical walkthrough: reading a report from start to finish

Select a report and identify its metadata

Choose a single committee report and note the committee name, committee ID, filing date and the reporting period. These items anchor every later check and help you find any related amendments or subsequent reports on FEC.gov FEC filing help pages.

Walk through schedule subtotals and reconcile totals

Open Schedule A and add the listed receipts or use the provided subtotal to compare to the total receipts line on the Form 3 or Form 3X. Repeat for Schedule B, then check Schedules C and D for loans and debts. If subtotals do not reconcile, check the form instructions for adjustments or omitted lines before drawing conclusions Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Red flags, verification steps and open questions for 2026

What to verify when precision matters

When exact figures matter, verify the report date, confirm the reporting period covers the transactions you are checking, and match schedule subtotals against the Form 3 or Form 3X summary lines. Consult the current form instructions for the precise itemization thresholds and calculation rules Form 3X: Committee and Other Party Committee Campaign Finance Report (Instructions).

Pending questions about formats and thresholds

Users should confirm whether any recent guidance changed itemization thresholds or electronic filing formats. The FEC help pages and form instruction notices are the authoritative place to check for updates that affect how schedules are reported FEC filing help pages.

How to cite FEC filings and responsibly use the data

Preferred citation practices

Cite the FEC PDF or the official data file with committee name and report date when you quote totals or specific entries. That practice makes it possible for readers to verify figures against the authoritative source FEC filing help pages.

When to link to the FEC PDF
