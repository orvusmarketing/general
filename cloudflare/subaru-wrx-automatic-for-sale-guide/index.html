What Subaru means by an 'automatic' WRX: the CVT explained

Subaru markets its automatic WRX option as a performance-oriented continuously variable transmission, sometimes described in materials as tuned for sport driving and fitted with simulated shift steps and paddle shifters to give a more familiar feel to drivers Edmunds WRX review

Technically, a CVT uses a variable ratio system rather than discrete gears, which can feel different from a torque-converter automatic; Subaru's configuration aims to reduce the 'rubber band' sensation by including simulated shifts and manual-shift controls so drivers can intervene when they want a more stepped feel MotorTrend on CVT vs manual

For everyday drivers this translates to easier operation in stop-and-go traffic and generally smooth acceleration, while many enthusiasts still favor the mechanical engagement of a six-speed manual for direct control and feedback Car and Driver review
