Performance, towing and daily driving: what to expect

Towing capacity differences and required equipment

The Sequoia offers substantially higher maximum towing capacity when properly equipped, while the 4Runner's towing capability is aligned with lighter towing needs. Proper towing requires specific equipment and sometimes dealer-installed options. Check the listing for a factory tow package, hitch receiver rating and cooling options that can change safe towing limits without altering the vehicle's basic appearance.

It depends on priorities: choose a Sequoia when you need three rows, larger cargo volume and higher towing capacity; choose a 4Runner when you favor lower entry price, off-road capability and strong resale in the midsize segment. Verify specific listings with VIN reports and pricing insights.

For daily driving, expect tradeoffs: the Sequoia's larger mass and powertrain choices tend to feel heavier at low speeds and consume more fuel, while the 4Runner's body-on-frame chassis delivers a more rugged off-road feel but can be less refined on pavement.
