What Keyword Surfer is and who makes it

keywordsurfer is a Chrome extension that injects lightweight keyword metrics directly into Google search results so operators can run quick, on-the-fly checks while they browse. The extension is distributed and documented by Surfer, which maintains the product and publishes its official overview on its site Keyword Surfer - free Chrome extension.

The extension is intended as a compact research aid rather than a full desktop research platform. It displays short, actionable signals near search results to speed triage during content work and planning without leaving the browser Keyword Surfer - Chrome Web Store listing.

What the free Chrome extension shows in Google Search

The free extension surfaces three core signals inline: estimated search volume for the term, a set of related keywords or terms for quick expansion, and an estimated cost-per-click figure where available. These appear near results or in a plugin panel depending on the Surfer UI version Keyword Surfer - free Chrome extension.

These numbers are presented as estimates to support exploration. Use them to prioritize ideas and create shortlists, not as final reporting metrics for budgets or attribution.
