What is Constitution Day in Mexico? Legal basis and purpose

What the law says, constitution day mexico

Constitution Day is a federal statutory rest day under the Ley Federal del Trabajo, established to commemorate the Constitution promulgated on February 5, and the law lists it among obligatory rest days for the country, under Article 74 of the labor code Ley Federal del Trabajo - Artículo 74.

The observance recognizes the adoption of the Constitution on February 5 and is treated as an official rest day by federal legal text, giving it a formal role beyond a commemorative event.

The Government of Mexico includes Constitution Day on its list of official rest days, which forms the baseline for employer obligations and public office closures Gobierno de México official rest days. You can also visit our constitutional rights hub for related context.

When is Constitution Day observed each year?

Nominal date versus observed day

The Constitution is dated February 5, but the legal observance is commonly moved to the first Monday of February so the calendar date and the observed rest day can differ from year to year, readers should note the difference when planning Ley Federal del Trabajo - Artículo 74. For published observance dates see a third party calendar such as Time and Date.

Moving the observance to the first Monday of February is a regular practice intended to consolidate the holiday into a weekday that reduces midweek disruptions, and because of that, the nominal date Feb 5 is not always the day off. What the law requires for workers and employers

Paid rest and compensatory pay rules

The Ley Federal del Trabajo entitles workers to paid rest on obligatory rest days and sets rules for additional pay if employees are required to work on those days, so employees who work on an obligatory rest day are generally due extra compensation under the statute Ley Federal del Trabajo - Artículo 74.

The Secretaría del Trabajo y Previsión Social lists obligatory rest days and provides baseline guidance on employer responsibilities, which employers use to determine pay and scheduling obligations for staff on those dates Días de descanso obligatorio, STPS.

Employer obligations and common exceptions

Collective agreements, employment contracts and specific business practices can change how an employer treats the day within the private sector, so the Ley Federal del Trabajo sets the baseline but does not eliminate workplace-level variations.

If an employer requires work on an obligatory rest day, the law defines compensatory pay rules, but details such as scheduling and substitutions can also be shaped by collective bargaining or company policy Días de descanso obligatorio, STPS. Schools and the academic calendar: what parents and students should know

How SEP publishes and implements calendars

The Secretaría de Educación Pública publishes each academic cycle's school calendar and implements the observance year-by-year, which means whether students have classes depends on the specific SEP calendar for that school cycle Calendario escolar, Secretaría de Educación Pública.

Private sector, contracts and collective agreements

Baseline legal commitments

The Ley Federal del Trabajo establishes the legal baseline for rest days but private employers may have different arrangements under employment contracts or collective agreements, which can affect whether staff receive the day off or special compensation Ley Federal del Trabajo - Artículo 74.

How contracts and agreements can differ

Employer notices, internal policies and collective bargaining agreements can change schedules or compensation beyond the statutory baseline, so workers should review their contract terms and any collective agreement that applies in their workplace Días de descanso obligatorio, STPS.

Common misunderstandings and typical pitfalls

Mistaking Feb 5 for the observed rest day

A common mistake is assuming Feb 5 is always the day off, when in many years the observance is moved to the first Monday of February, so check the official calendar rather than planning around the nominal date Ley Federal del Trabajo - Artículo 74.

Assuming private employers must follow public-sector closures

Another frequent error is assuming private companies must close when public offices do, the statutory baseline exists, but employer-specific contracts and collective agreements can change whether a private worker has the day off Días de descanso obligatorio, STPS.
