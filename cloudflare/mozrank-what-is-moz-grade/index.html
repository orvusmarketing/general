What people mean by "Moz grade" and where MozRank fits

Definition and common uses

The term Moz grade is often used informally to describe a page or domain's link-popularity score, commonly reported as MozRank, and operators use it as a shorthand for link-profile strength. MozRank is Moz's link-popularity metric presented on a roughly 0 to 10 scale to reflect the quality and quantity of inbound links to a page, as described in Moz's documentation Moz Learn page on MozRank

MozRank is derived from the web link graph and applies weighted value from linking pages on a compressed, roughly logarithmic scale. The precise formula and weightings are proprietary to Moz, so the metric is best read as a relative indicator rather than an exact measure you can reverse engineer, which Moz also notes in its product guidance Moz Link Explorer

Practitioners use Moz grade as a quick comparator when auditing link profiles, combining it with other Moz metrics and analytics data to prioritise work, and reference the Orvus blog for further practical notes on audits Orvus blog.

Common uses include prioritising outreach targets, checking whether earned links come from pages with higher link value, and tracking relative progress across campaigns. Because the underlying computation is proprietary, most teams focus on repeatable link-acquisition processes and measuring traffic or conversions from referral sources rather than chasing the numeric score.

How Moz calculates MozRank and how it relates to other Moz metrics

What the 0-10 scale represents

MozRank reports link popularity on an approximately 0-10 scale that compresses raw link graph signals so differences at the high end are harder to move than at the low end. This compression behaves like a logarithmic transformation of link value and linking-source quality, which is how Moz presents the concept in its help material Moz Learn page on MozRank

Relation to Domain Authority and Page Authority

MozRank is one part of Moz's broader metric set that also includes Domain Authority and Page Authority, which aggregate signals differently to estimate domain- or page-level ranking potential. Moz recommends reading these metrics together to get context on link-profile strength rather than relying on a single number Moz Help overview of Moz metrics (see an explainer on Domain Authority at Segment SEO Segment SEO).

For practical diagnostics, use MozRank to assess link popularity at the page level, and use Domain Authority to compare larger domain-level trends. Page Authority provides a complementary view of a specific page's likely performance. Combining them helps avoid decisions based on one transient value.

How to check mozrank for pages and domains

Tools: Link Explorer and MozBar

The primary ways to view MozRank and related Moz metrics are Moz's Link Explorer product and the MozBar browser extension, both of which surface MozRank, Domain Authority, and Page Authority for pages and domains Moz Link Explorer and see Moz's help for Link Explorer Moz Link Explorer help.

Practitioners usually mean MozRank or a related composite Moz metric when they say Moz grade. Use it as a relative link-popularity signal alongside Domain Authority, Page Authority, and analytics to prioritise link work and measure outcomes rather than treating it as a sole proof of success.

Practical checks and cadence

When checking mozrank, view both page-level MozRank and domain summaries. Look at referral traffic alongside Moz metrics to see whether links are producing measurable visits.

Keep expectations realistic: observed score changes depend on the rate of new links your site receives and Moz's index refresh cadence. Because Moz updates its index on its own schedule, small link changes may take time to appear in reported scores.

How to interpret mozrank: decision criteria and what to prioritize

When mozrank matters for site decisions

Treat mozrank as a relative comparator for link-profile strength and for tracking campaign progress, not as standalone proof of SEO success. Using the metric in this way helps teams prioritise link targets without overfitting to a single number Moz Help overview of Moz metrics

Combine mozrank with referral traffic and conversion metrics to connect link work to outcomes. When links bring useful referral visits, they offer operational value regardless of small score movements.

When to look beyond the score

Look beyond mozrank when a page has mismatched signals, for example high MozRank but low referral traffic, or when short-term changes might reflect crawl or index timing rather than sustained link value. In those cases, check analytics and source quality before reallocating resources Moz Link Explorer

Focus on reproducible link acquisition processes and measurement that ties links to traffic or revenue, rather than optimising exclusively for a single metric.

Practical ways teams can influence mozrank

Link acquisition and link quality

Improving mozrank depends on acquiring links from higher-quality sources. Link source quality matters because Moz's metric weights linking pages when calculating link value, so links from authoritative pages tend to move the needle more than many low-quality links Moz Learn page on MozRank

In practical terms, prioritise outreach and content that are likely to earn links from relevant, better-regarded sites. Track source quality in your link acquisition workflow so you can prioritise targets that are more likely to improve link signal quality over time (see our services Orvus services).

Internal linking and cleanup

Good internal linking and sensible site structure help pass link value around a site and make it easier for crawlers to find pages that should benefit from inbound links. Internal links do not replace external link value, but they help distribute the benefit across important pages.

Removing or disavowing spammy or clearly low-quality links can be a sensible cleanup step when a site has a pattern of harmful linking. Common mistakes and pitfalls when tracking Moz metrics

Over-reliance on a single score

A common mistake is treating mozrank as a final verdict on link health. Because MozRank is one signal among several, combining it with Domain Authority, Page Authority, referral traffic, and conversion metrics gives more robust context for decisions Moz Help overview of Moz metrics

Teams that chase score changes without tying link work to measurable outcomes risk spending effort on low-impact activities. Keep tests and measurement focused on visits and conversions where possible.

Misreading short-term fluctuations

Short-term Moz metric fluctuations can reflect crawl timing or index refresh cycles rather than real changes in link value, which leads to reactive decisions. To avoid this, document your checks and allow time for patterns to stabilise before acting Moz Link Explorer

Also avoid measurement mistakes such as ignoring referral traffic or failing to record link-acquisition processes, both of which make it harder to connect link effort to outcomes Search Engine Journal analysis of MozRank and PageRank
