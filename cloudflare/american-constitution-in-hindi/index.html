Quick answer: What the Constitution of America is formally called

The document's formal English name is "The Constitution of the United States of America," most commonly shortened to the United States Constitution, as shown in the authoritative public transcript from the National Archives National Archives transcription.

Using the exact official title matters for citation and clarity, especially if you are pairing an English primary text with a Hindi rendering for study or classroom use, because translations are treated as educational renderings rather than official federal texts Constitution Annotated.

Historical origin and brief timeline of the Constitution

The Constitution was drafted during the Philadelphia Constitutional Convention in 1787; delegates debated structure, powers, and procedures before producing the document that would be submitted to the states for ratification National Archives transcription.

After state ratification in 1788 the Constitution went into effect in 1789, and the first ten amendments, known as the Bill of Rights, were ratified in 1791; these dates and the ratification sequence are recorded in authoritative accounts and annotated texts Constitution Annotated.

How the Constitution is organized: Preamble, Articles, and Amendments

The document opens with a short Preamble stating general purposes, followed by seven Articles that set out the structure of the federal government and the relationships between the national government and the states; this conventional division appears in public transcripts and educational materials Constitution Annotated.

The seven Articles cover core topics such as the legislative branch, the executive branch, the judicial branch, interstate relations, amendment procedures, federal authority, and the process for ratification; later Amendments, including the Bill of Rights, are appended to that structure in the published texts National Archives transcription.

For readers, the Preamble serves as an introductory statement of purposes rather than an operative legal section, while the Articles and Amendments contain the operative clauses and procedural rules that are cited in legal and historical work Legal Information Institute. Typical mistakes and pitfalls when looking for the Constitution in Hindi

A common mistake is assuming that any Hindi text labeled "Constitution" is an official government translation; many are unpaid or volunteer renderings and may omit nuance or use paraphrase rather than literal wording National Archives transcription.

Another pitfall is relying on summaries, commentaries, or classroom glosses in place of the English primary text; summaries can shift emphasis or use modern phrasing that changes legal meaning, so always pair a Hindi explanation with the English original when accuracy matters Constitution Annotated.

Practical examples: how to cite the Constitution and a Hindi rendering
