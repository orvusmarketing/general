Practical maintenance steps that can lower ownership costs

Owners can safely perform simple tasks that reduce shop visits: check fluid levels, replace air and cabin filters, inspect belts and hoses, and maintain recommended oil change intervals. These routine tasks help prevent accelerated wear and are documented in manufacturer guidance CarFax Deals overview.

When parts or labor are costly, compare reputable aftermarket and OEM parts for price and warranty. Sourcing parts carefully and timing service around routine intervals often lowers overall spend compared with unscheduled repairs RepairPal maintenance overview.

Prioritize repairs that prevent larger failures, such as fixing a small coolant leak before it leads to head-gasket or overheating issues. Use service history to find cars that have regular scheduled maintenance documented CarFax Deals overview.

A buyer's checklist for inspecting a used MX-5 before you contact the dealer

Before you visit, get the vehicle history report and review title status, ownership history, and service records. A full service history and single-owner reports reduce the probability of deferred maintenance CarFax Deals overview.

On the test drive and at the lot, inspect the soft-top for tears or leaking seals, feel the clutch engagement and listen for suspension noises, check for oil or coolant leaks under the car, and confirm no warning lights are present RepairPal maintenance overview.
