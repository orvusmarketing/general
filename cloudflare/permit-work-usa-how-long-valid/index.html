Short answer: how long is a U.S. work permit valid?

A U.S. work permit does not have one single validity period. Whether your authorization is an Employment Authorization Document that USCIS issues, or a visa-based authorization tied to an employer petition, the dates that matter will appear on the agency approval or visa documents and vary by category and case.

What an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) is and when people use it

An Employment Authorization Document, commonly called an EAD, is the USCIS document that shows an individual may work in the United States for the dates printed on the card. The card itself and the USCIS approval notice record those dates, and USCIS explains the application process on its Form I-765 page Form I-765 page.

People obtain EADs under many eligibility categories, from adjustment of status applicants to certain humanitarian categories. USCIS provides an overview of EAD eligibility categories and the kinds of applicants who normally file Form I-765 on its EAD information pages USCIS EAD overview. STEM OPT applicants may also consult specialized FAQs such as the STEM OPT guidance STEM OPT FAQ.

Some workers do not use an EAD because their authorization comes from an employer-sponsored visa petition. Those visa-based work rules are handled through the Department of State and USCIS guidance for specific visa classes Department of State visa validity guidance.

How USCIS sets and communicates EAD validity periods

USCIS records the exact start and end dates of an EAD on both the card and in the approval notice; applicants should read the approval notice carefully to know the authorized period and any conditions that apply Form I-765 page.

Validity lengths are not uniform. USCIS assigns dates based on the applicant's eligibility category and case facts, and those category rules are explained in the Form I-765 instructions and EAD guidance USCIS EAD overview.

Why EAD lengths differ by category and what that means for planning

USCIS issues EADs with varied validity lengths that reflect the legal category under which the applicant qualifies. That means two people applying under different categories may receive different periods of authorization even if they file at the same time; the Form I-765 guidance explains these category distinctions Form I-765 page.

Before making employment or travel plans, applicants should check the EAD overview for category-specific notes and the Form I-765 instructions so they understand the requirements tied to their eligibility category USCIS EAD overview.

Renewal timing and the automatic extension rule

When an EAD holder needs to renew, Form I-765 is the official application to seek continued work authorization; USCIS posts step-by-step filing instructions, fee information, and category checklists on the Form I-765 page Form I-765 page. Work permit validity depends on the type of authorization, with EAD dates shown on the card and approval notice and visa-based work periods governed by employer petitions and Department of State visa validity; check the USCIS Form I-765 page and DOS visa guidance for the authoritative dates.

Certain timely-filed I-765 renewals may trigger a limited automatic extension of employment authorization, commonly up to 180 days where the rule applies, but applicants must confirm eligibility and required evidence on the USCIS automatic extension alert USCIS automatic extension alert.

To preserve eligibility for any automatic extension, assemble required evidence early, including a copy of your current EAD, the completed Form I-765, photos, and fee or fee-waiver documentation as listed on USCIS pages Form I-765 page.

Employer-sponsored visas and how their validity differs from an EAD

Nonimmigrant, employer-sponsored work authorizations such as H-1B and L-1 rely on the approved employer petition and the Department of State visa validity to determine authorized work dates rather than an EAD card; check DOS guidance for visa validity rules Department of State visa validity guidance.

Replacing a lost, stolen, or damaged EAD

If your EAD card is lost or damaged, file Form I-765 following the replacement instructions on the USCIS page and include the evidence USCIS lists for replacement requests Form I-765 page.

Replacement filings may require proof of identity, a copy of the lost card if available, and possibly a police report in specific situations; check USCIS guidance and the processing-times page for expected waits on replacement requests USCIS processing-times page.
