Quick answer and why reliability matters

The short answer from owner-based datasets and repair-cost analyses is that Lexus models, and in particular the Lexus RX among mid-size SUVs, consistently rank at or near the top of reliability lists.

This pattern appears across multiple large studies and owner surveys that track problems and repair trends over time, which makes reliability a practical filter for used buyers who want fewer surprises and a clearer estimate of total cost to own. J.D. Power Vehicle Dependability Study

Reliability matters because vehicles with lower reported repair frequency tend to need fewer unexpected visits to the shop, and that usually lowers running costs and supports steadier resale value. Consider repair frequency and average repair cost together when comparing models, since a low number of visits is less helpful if those visits are very expensive.

Always set expectations: rankings change by model year, trim and local market supply, and a dependable model does not remove the need for a VIN-level check and an independent inspection before purchase.

Different studies measure reliability in different ways, and that makes a difference when you compare results. Understanding methodologies helps you weigh which ranking matters most for your priorities.

J.D. Power VDS - problems per 100 vehicles (PP100)

J.D. Power's Vehicle Dependability Study reports problems per 100 vehicles over a defined ownership window and uses lower PP100 as the sign of higher dependability. That approach focuses on real owner-reported problems over the first three years of ownership and is useful when you want a sense of common issues across a brand or model. J.D. Power Vehicle Dependability Study

Consumer Reports' owner surveys and composite scores

Consumer Reports builds composite reliability scores from large owner-survey panels combined with road-test results to reflect real-world durability and owner experience. This method weights many small owner reports and test results to produce a long-term reliability view that can differ from short-term problem counts. Consumer Reports reliability summary

RepairPal's repair frequency and cost approach

RepairPal combines repair frequency, severity and average repair cost to estimate which models are least likely to require costly service. That cost-focused perspective complements owner-survey metrics and can change model rankings when repair costs, not just visit counts, drive ownership expense. RepairPal reliability ratings

Each measurement style has value. Use owner-survey counts to spot common problems, composite scores to understand long-term durability, and repair-cost analyses to estimate likely spending on maintenance and repairs.

What to check on a VIN-level history report

Start with title status to confirm there are no salvage or branded titles. Then verify ownership history for mileage consistency and check for service records that indicate regular maintenance. These items influence future repair likelihood and can change the model's expected total cost to own.

Also use documented service history to confirm timely maintenance. A well maintained vehicle with regular service entries tends to be less risky than one with gaps, all else equal.
