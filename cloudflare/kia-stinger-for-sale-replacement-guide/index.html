Why Kia stopped selling the Stinger in the U.S. and what that means

Kia's official model archive and public statements

Kia ended U.S. new-car sales of the Stinger after the 2023 model year, removing the model from its U.S. catalog; this change is reflected on Kia's official vehicle pages and archive Kia USA model archive

That removal means there is no direct new gasoline Stinger replacement sold by Kia in the U.S. through 2026. Buyers who want the Stinger driving character therefore must either look at comparable new sedans, consider EV performance models, or search the used market for existing Stingers.

Kia has signaled a performance direction that emphasizes high output EV trims like the EV6 GT as part of the brand's future performance lineup rather than launching a new gasoline GT sedan in the U.S. market EV6 and EV6 GT | Kia USA

The strategic shift matters for buyers because it changes which models the company will prioritize for development, marketing, and dealer support. If a buyer's priority is a new gasoline rear-wheel oriented performance sedan, Kia's U.S. lineup does not currently offer a direct replacement, which pushes many shoppers toward used examples or to consider other model types.
