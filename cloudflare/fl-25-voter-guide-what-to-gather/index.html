Reading campaign finance: how to use FEC records for FL-25 candidates

Search the FEC data portal for a candidate name or committee to find committee filings, contribution reports, and receipt summaries. The portal is the authoritative source for 2026 committee activity and contribution records FEC data portal

Look for the following record types in each filing: periodic reports that list receipts and disbursements, reports of independent expenditures, and any supplemental filings that correct earlier totals. Note the report date so you are reading the most recent 2026 filings.

Use the portal's filters to isolate year and committee type. If a summary table shows totals, click through to the detailed report to confirm contributor types and major receipts rather than relying on a single headline number.

Remember common limitations: FEC reports are official but may be amended, and timing matters. Always record the filing date and, if available, the report's PDF to preserve the precise record you inspected.

Quick checks for registration and voting steps

Check state deadlines before voting

Confirm your polling place and ID requirements at the state lookup page. If you have moved or changed your name, update your registration well before the published deadline and keep a record of the confirmation page or email.

Plan absentee and early voting timelines based on the state's published ranges. If you intend to request a mail ballot, note the submission method and the deadline for receiving or returning the ballot in 2026 to avoid disqualification.

Using district data and issue indicators to contextualize candidate statements

Use the American Community Survey and related district-level products to build a demographic baseline for FL-25. These datasets provide age, income, housing, and commute patterns that are useful when comparing candidate priorities to district needs American Community Survey

Example indicators to check include median household income, percentage of residents over 65, commuting modes, and employment sectors. Pick indicators that align with the topics candidates emphasize so you can evaluate whether a stated priority addresses a measurable district issue.

Avoid over-claiming from raw statistics. Use the ACS as a cited source when quoting figures and note the survey year because district composition may be affected by redistricting or demographic change over time.
