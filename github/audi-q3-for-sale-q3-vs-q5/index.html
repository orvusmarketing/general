Common listing mistakes and traps to avoid

Avoid assuming the listed model year always equals the equipment level; trims and option codes often change what a vehicle includes. Check for mismatched option descriptions in the photos and the listing text before you visit.

Do not ignore ownership and service history signals. Gaps in service records, multiple short-term owners or title issues in the vehicle history report can indicate higher maintenance risk even if the price looks fair.

Quick checks to rule out problems include running a VIN check for title status, confirming odometer consistency across the vehicle history report, and asking the dealer for service invoices that match the reported records.

How to evaluate a specific listing: inspection and question checklist

VIN-based checks to run before a visit: vehicle history report, title status, reported accident history, service records and mileage check. Save or print the report and bring it to the inspection so you can compare reported events with the vehicle's visible condition.

Questions to ask the dealer on first contact: is this a certified-preowned vehicle, can you show recent service invoices, how many owners has it had and are there any open recalls. Ask for the exact trim designation and option list to confirm cargo and equipment claims on the listing.

During the test drive and inspection, verify the listed options match the vehicle, check for uneven tire wear and listen for unusual noises. If the service history shows recurring repairs on the same component, get a mechanic's opinion on likely short term maintenance needs.
