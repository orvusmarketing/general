How U.S. government rules and publications determine wait times

Difference between USCIS processing times and the DOS Visa Bulletin

Two separate official systems determine most wait time components: USCIS posts form- and service-center-specific processing-time estimates, which reflect how long adjudicators take to process a form, and the Department of State publishes the monthly Visa Bulletin, which controls whether a numerically limited visa is available by priority date, as explained on the USCIS processing page USCIS processing times.

The Visa Bulletin establishes final action and filing dates for family- and employment-preference categories and therefore shapes when an applicant with a priority date can proceed to adjustment or consular processing, according to the Department of State Visa Bulletin.

How premium processing and concurrent filing affect speed

What premium processing does and does not change

Premium processing reduces adjudication time for certain petitions, like many I-140 filings, but USCIS notes that it does not affect Visa Bulletin priority-date backlogs or the timing of adjustment or consular appointments USCIS premium processing page.

Using premium processing can shorten the waiting window for a petition decision, which sometimes helps when a priority date is about to become current, but remember that premium processing does not guarantee faster overall final action if the Visa Bulletin remains behind your date USCIS processing times.

When concurrent filing is permitted and helpful

Concurrent filing of an I-140 and I-485 is allowed in situations where the applicant's priority date is current and can meaningfully reduce calendar time by filing the adjustment application while the petition is pending, but eligibility rules vary and the USCIS processing pages should be checked for specifics USCIS processing times.
