Quick answer: how many American Job Centers are there right now?

The commonly cited national baseline is nearly 2,400 American Job Centers, a public-facing figure maintained by CareerOneStop as the quickest reference for the public to use when searching for services.

That number is useful as a first answer, but it is a starting point for verification because state lists and local boards can add, consolidate or rename sites at different times.

For an immediate search, use the CareerOneStop AJC locator to confirm the nearest site by ZIP code or city.

What are American Job Centers and who runs them?

American Job Centers are local workforce service sites that act as one-stop access points for job search help, career counseling, training referrals and referrals to related state programs, as defined under the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act, or WIOA.

The Employment and Training Administration at the U.S. Department of Labor provides the program framework and operating guidance that describe what AJCs do and how states and local workforce boards should oversee them, which helps explain why counts appear in both federal and state resources.

States and local workforce boards operate and maintain the actual centers, which means a national portal and a state directory both play roles in locating service points.

Why there is no single perfect national roster

Counts differ because states update directories on different schedules, local offices sometimes consolidate multiple service points under a single center name, and some entries represent virtual-only service points rather than brick-and-mortar offices.

CareerOneStop provides a useful national inventory, but timing and naming choices at the state or local level can produce differences between the national list and a state directory.

When publishing totals, it is best to record the access date for each state page so readers understand when the count was verified.

To build a verified state table, begin with the CareerOneStop national list and then open each state workforce agency directory to confirm the list of local centers and any local naming conventions.

When you check a state page, record the access date and note whether an entry covers several service points, is virtual-only, or is clearly a single brick-and-mortar office.

Common reasons counts differ and how to resolve specific cases

Shared facilities can be listed as one center on a state page but appear as multiple service points in a national inventory, depending on how the local board names and reports sites.

Virtual-only service points add another layer of ambiguity because some researchers include them in a total while others count only physical locations; be explicit in your method about which you include.

When an entry is ambiguous, a practical resolution is to count named brick-and-mortar addresses as centers, flag virtual entries separately, and note any consolidated or regional listings that require follow-up with a local workforce board.

What services you can expect at an American Job Center

Under WIOA guidance, AJCs are intended to provide job search assistance, career counseling, training referrals, and connections to other workforce programs, though the exact mix of on-site services varies by location.

Because services differ locally, check the center webpage or the state directory entry for a given location to confirm what staff offer in person and whether specialized programs such as training grants or apprenticeship referrals are available.

Mistakes to avoid when searching for a local AJC

A common mistake is relying on an out-of-date local webpage or a third-party listing without checking the state directory and recording the access date to confirm the listing is current.

Another error is confusing partner organizations or co-located programs with an official AJC listing; the state directory is the authoritative source for whether a site is officially part of the AJC network.

If in doubt about a listing, call the phone number shown on the state page before making plans to visit in person.
