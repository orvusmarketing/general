Common research mistakes and how to avoid them

Mistakes that lead to misinformation

Common errors include relying only on campaign messaging, accepting secondary summaries as definitive, and treating unsourced social posts as factual. These mistakes can create inaccurate timelines or inflated claims about finance and ballot status Sun Sentinel coverage.

Another frequent issue is confusing committee names when a campaign uses similar legal names across different filings. Always check the exact committee registration on FEC pages to avoid mixing records FEC candidate search.
