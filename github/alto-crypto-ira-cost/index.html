Definition and context: what is an Alto IRA and how does crypto fit in

AltoIRA is a platform for self-directed IRAs that explicitly supports alternative assets such as private equity, real estate, and cryptocurrencies, letting account owners hold nontraditional investments inside tax-advantaged retirement accounts. Pricing 2D AltoIRA

Self-directed IRAs differ from standard brokerage IRAs because they let you direct custody and investment into alternatives, but that flexibility changes where fees appear. Alto documents its pricing categories as account or administrative fees, per-asset custody or transaction fees, and optional advisor fees, which helps separate what Alto charges from what third-party custodians charge. Pricing 2D AltoIRA

Crypto fits into this model differently than stocks or ETFs because Alto routes crypto custody through third-party crypto custodians and wallet providers. Those custodians operate independent fee schedules for custody, transfers, and trading, which are separate from Alto's account fees. Crypto investing with Alto

How Alto's fee model is structured

At a high level, Alto emphasizes flat administrative or account fees rather than charging a default percentage-of-assets management fee. This means some basic costs are predictable, while asset-level costs depend on what you hold. Alto IRA Review (see Milk Road's review)

The next major line items are per-asset custody or transaction charges that apply to nontraditional holdings. These fees vary by asset class and by the custodian or service that actually holds the asset. Alto lists these categories so you can see which fees come from Alto and which come from custodians. Crypto inside an Alto IRA: where extra costs come from

Alto routes crypto custody through third-party custodians and wallet services, so crypto inside an Alto account generally incurs extra custody and transaction fees beyond Alto's account fees. Check the third-party custodian disclosures to see exact charges. Crypto investing with Alto

Third-party crypto custodians and wallets

Custodians that hold crypto often charge wallet custody fees, per-transfer fees, or storage fees that apply while assets remain in the account. These are separate legal and operational relationships from Alto's administrative role. How crypto IRAs work 2D custody and fees explained

Trading spreads, custody fees, and alto crypto ira considerations

Crypto trading often comes with spreads or per-trade charges set by dealers or execution partners, which can widen the effective cost compared with trading an ETF on an exchange. These trading spreads and per-trade costs are typically described in the custodian or dealer disclosures. How crypto IRAs work 2D custody and fees explained

Rollovers and transfer costs to and from Alto

Decision criteria: when Alto's model makes sense for you

Alto's model, with a flat admin fee plus per-asset custody charges, can be cost-effective if you hold a small number of concentrated alternative assets where per-asset custody is predictable. Compare that profile with how many distinct assets you will hold. Alto IRA Review

It can be for some strategies, particularly if you hold a small number of concentrated alternative assets and confirm custodian fees in advance, but many small crypto positions or frequent trading can increase costs; verify all fees on Alto's pricing page and the custodian disclosures before deciding.

If you plan many small crypto positions, per-asset custody and trading spreads can add up and make the account less economical than a simpler custody arrangement.
