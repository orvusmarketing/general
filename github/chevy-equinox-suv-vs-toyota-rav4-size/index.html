Wheelbase and passenger space: rear-seat comfort and legroom

How wheelbase affects interior space

Wheelbase measurements published for recent model years typically show the Equinox with a longer wheelbase than the RAV4, and a longer wheelbase often translates into marginally more rear-seat legroom because there is more distance between the axles to package occupant space Chevrolet specifications

However, wheelbase is only one factor for passenger comfort; seat cushion length, seatback angle, and trim-specific seating layouts also play a role, so a longer wheelbase does not automatically mean a substantially roomier rear seat in all trims Car and Driver comparison carsized comparison

What reviews report about rear-seat legroom

Measurement-based reviews often note that the Equinox can feel a bit more generous in rear-seat legroom in certain trims, consistent with the wheelbase advantage reported in specification tables Edmunds comparison

Because reviews compare specific trims and seat configurations, use them to see how rear-seat space measures up for the exact trim you are considering rather than relying only on the base model figures Car and Driver comparison

How trim and powertrain change the picture

Hybrid vs non-hybrid RAV4 and Equinox variants

Hybrid powertrains and different spare tire or battery placements can change cargo volume and interior packaging so a RAV4 hybrid or a well equipped Equinox trim could shift the relative advantage on cargo or passenger space versus base models Toyota specifications

A practical checklist to use when comparing listings

Immediate checks before contacting a dealer

Confirm model year and exact trim, compare published cargo volume and wheelbase on the manufacturer site, check title status and request a vehicle history report, and verify that photos show the cargo area and rear seats Chevrolet specifications

Note the mileage and service history as context for total cost of ownership, even though those do not affect physical size, and save screenshots of the listing and manufacturer spec page for comparison Toyota specifications

Documentation and photos to request

Request photos of the cargo area with and without the rear seats folded, the sticker or build sheet showing exact trim and options, and pictures of seat adjustment ranges to assess real rear-seat comfort Edmunds comparison

If the spare tire or battery location is not visible in photos, ask whether the vehicle has a temporary tire kit or a full size spare because that can affect usable cargo height and floor flatness Kelley Blue Book cargo comparison

When to set a test drive and what to measure in person

In-person measurements that matter

Bring a sample item such as a stroller or a bike wheel and measure rear-seat legroom, cargo opening width, and the flat floor length with seats folded to confirm real world fit rather than relying only on published liters Car and Driver comparison

Check seat adjustment range, headroom with typical passengers, and whether seats fold flat enough for the items you need to carry, because practical flexibility matters more than a small difference in headline numbers Edmunds comparison

Questions to ask the dealer or seller

Ask for the build code or window sticker, whether the vehicle has a full size spare, and whether any aftermarket cargo accessories are installed that might affect usable space or load height Chevrolet specifications

Also confirm how the seller reports options such as towing packages, roof rails, or a cargo management system because those options can change how you pack and use the cargo area Toyota specifications

Conclusion: practical next steps and final comparison takeaways

Summary of main differences

In summary, manufacturer specifications and measurement comparisons show the Equinox is generally slightly longer and often has a longer wheelbase, while the RAV4 typically offers more usable cargo volume; width and height are similar so parking and garage fit are usually comparable Edmunds comparison

These are general tendencies and trim or powertrain choices can reverse or narrow the differences, so confirm the exact trim specific specs and, where useful, run a vehicle history report before arranging a visit to the seller Chevrolet specifications

Published wheelbase figures typically favor the Equinox and some reviews note marginally more rear-seat legroom in certain trims, but seat design and trim options also matter, so check trim-specific rear-seat measurements or test in person.

Measurement-based reviews and published cargo figures usually show the RAV4 provides more usable cargo volume behind the rear seats, though some Equinox trims can narrow that gap, so verify the listing's cargo specs.
