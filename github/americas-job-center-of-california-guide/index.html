Definition: What the America's Job Center of California is

America's Job Center of California refers to the state's network of career and workforce hubs that operate as part of the national One-Stop/American Job Center system under the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act, and it connects local services with statewide online tools.

AJCC sites combine in-person assistance and online access to job listings, training referrals and unemployment-related resources, and the state links those centers to CalJOBS as a recommended starting place for job seekers America's Job Center of California (AJCC) - A summary for job seekers.

At a national level, the AJC or One-Stop network is the federal-state-local system that sets expectations for services such as career counseling, employer engagement and staff-assisted training referrals under WIOA Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) and American Job Centers.

How the AJCC network is organized in California

The Employment Development Department administers the America's Job Center of California network at the state level and connects local centers to statewide tools and reporting systems, while local workforce boards manage many day-to-day service partnerships America's Job Center of California (AJCC) - A summary for job seekers.

Local workforce boards typically coordinate referrals to training, apprenticeship programs and employer partnerships, and they work with AJCC sites to align services with regional labor market needs.

Many centers offer both physical locations and virtual help, and CalJOBS is the primary online link that lets job seekers register, search listings and schedule appointments with staff CalJOBS - California's online jobs and services portal.

Because governance is shared across state, local and federal actors, center operations can look different from one county to the next; the EDD provides statewide standards while local boards tailor services.

Services you can expect at an AJCC

AJCCs provide a mix of self-service tools and staff-assisted services; self-service options include online job search, resume posting and skills assessments, while staff-assisted services involve career counseling and individualized training referrals American Job Centers (AJC) overview.

Common service categories include career counseling, resume and interview help, assessment of skills, access to training programs and apprenticeships, employer matching, and connections to unemployment or veteran services America's Job Center of California (AJCC) - A summary for job seekers.

Find a local AJCC, create a CalJOBS account, prepare documents like ID and resume, and schedule an appointment for staff-assisted services if you need individualized guidance.

Staff-assisted services are typically reserved for registered clients who need individualized planning, while many basic tools remain available online for anyone to use; federal guidance expects AJCCs to offer both options Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) and American Job Centers.

Because offerings vary by site, check the local center page or call ahead to confirm which services are available in person and whether language access or veteran-specific supports are offered.

Using CalJOBS: step-by-step for job seekers

CalJOBS is California's official portal for job seekers and employers to search openings, post resumes and register for AJCC services, and it is the recommended place to start when you plan to use AJCC staff assistance CalJOBS - California's online jobs and services portal.

First, create a CalJOBS account using the registration guidance on the EDD site. Fill in your profile so staff can match you to openings and programs Step 3: Register in CalJOBS - EDD.

Next, build or upload a resume, search job listings and save positions you want to apply to. Use the portal's employer-matching tools to see openings that align with your skills.

Finally, register for services through CalJOBS and schedule an in-person or virtual appointment with a local AJCC if you need staff-assisted guidance or program referrals.

How AJCCs connect people to training, apprenticeships and employers

Local AJCCs refer job seekers to training programs and registered apprenticeships through established partnerships with community colleges, training providers and employer consortia; local workforce boards often manage these relationships America's Job Center of California (AJCC) - A summary for job seekers.

Referral pathways typically begin with an assessment and may include direct enrollment in short-term training, connection to apprenticeship sponsors or a referral to employer-sponsored upskilling programs.

Employers can post jobs and participate in on-site recruitment or screening events at AJCC locations, and CalJOBS also provides employer-facing tools for posting and applicant matching CalJOBS - California's online jobs and services portal.

Because local workforce boards coordinate many training agreements, the types of credential or apprenticeship available will depend on regional labor needs and partnerships.

How to choose the right AJCC or service for your situation

Decide based on services offered, program specialties, hours and language access. Look for veteran services, specific training partners and whether the center offers virtual appointments CalJOBS - California's online jobs and services portal.
