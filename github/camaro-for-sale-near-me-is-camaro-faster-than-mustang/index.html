Straight-line performance: 0-60 and quarter-mile test results

Independent 0-60 and quarter-mile tests show consistent patterns: Camaro ZL1-class trims with supercharged V8s often post quicker straight-line times than standard Mustang GT trims in comparable tests, though exact numbers depend on model year and tire or traction setup Car and Driver Camaro ZL1 first test. Those independent runs also highlight that published horsepower is only part of the story. Factors such as curb weight, gearing, traction control calibration and tire choice materially affect measured sprint times, which is why two cars with similar horsepower can produce different 0-60 or quarter-mile results Road & Track performance testing.

Because of these variables, independent test results are useful as benchmarks but not as guarantees for any single used example. A well-equipped, track-prepared Mustang variant may outpace a poorly maintained or heavier Camaro of the same era, so treat published test numbers as context rather than definitive proof about a specific listing MotorTrend trim-by-trim comparison.

When reading test results, pay attention to the test conditions described by the reviewer: altitude, temperature, tire brand and the presence of launch control or extra cooling. These notes explain why two independent runs can differ for what appears to be the same model and trim Car and Driver Camaro ZL1 first test.

For shoppers, the key takeaway is this: use independent 0-60 and quarter-mile results to prioritize trims and to set expectations, but verify the actual vehicle's build and condition with a VIN-based report before relying on those numbers for a purchase decision.
