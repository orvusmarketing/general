What "nextdoor neighbor" visibility means: public posts, neighborhood posts, private messages

The term nextdoor neighbor in everyday use refers to how your profile and posts appear within the service and to people nearby. According to Nextdoor's Privacy Policy, the company distinguishes public posts, neighborhood-only posts, and private messages, and it explains that public posts are generally visible beyond a single neighborhood Nextdoor Privacy Policy

Nextdoor separates public posts, neighborhood-only posts, and private messages; the company retains those records and states it will disclose content in response to lawful subpoenas, warrants, or emergency requests, as summarized in its privacy policy and transparency reporting.

Neighborhood-only posts are designed to be seen by verified neighbors and local moderators and are not open to the general public, but the company stores those posts and may disclose them under lawful process, as Nextdoor's help documentation makes clear Nextdoor Help Center

Private messages are not displayed publicly on the site, but like neighborhood posts they are retained by the platform and can be provided to third parties when Nextdoor is served with valid legal process, according to the company's policy Nextdoor Privacy Policy

How Nextdoor stores and shares content behind the scenes

Nextdoor explains that neighborhood posts and private messages are stored as part of normal platform operations and that copies are retained for operational and legal reasons; the privacy policy describes the categories of information the company may keep and how it handles requests for that information Nextdoor Privacy Policy

Within a neighborhood, verified neighbors and local moderators can read neighborhood posts, and those roles are separate from public viewers outside the neighborhood; the help center outlines how verification and moderator access work for neighborhood content Nextdoor Help Center

Retention by the company does not mean content is publicly available, but retained records may be supplied to law enforcement or other authorities when Nextdoor receives lawful process, so internal access and external disclosure are distinct matters in the platform's documentation Nextdoor Privacy Policy

Can police get Nextdoor posts? How law enforcement requests are handled

Nextdoor states that it responds to lawful legal process such as subpoenas, warrants, and some emergency requests and that when served with such orders it may disclose stored content including posts and messages Nextdoor Privacy Policy

The company also publishes a transparency report that aggregates counts and high-level descriptions of government and law enforcement requests and of the disclosures Nextdoor made in response, which helps show how often platforms receive formal requests Nextdoor Transparency Report 2024. Practical steps neighbors can take to reduce what police could access

Start by reviewing account privacy and post audience settings and remove unnecessary address details from your profile; Nextdoor's privacy settings pages show step-by-step actions such as adjusting who can see posts and what profile fields are visible Nextdoor Help Center and you can review our privacy page for related guidance.

Keep posts factual and avoid sharing sensitive personal identifiers or exact home details in neighborhood posts or messages, and prefer neighborhood-only posts when appropriate because casual public visibility is greater for public posts Nextdoor Privacy Policy

Understanding legal differences: subpoenas, warrants, emergency disclosures

A subpoena is typically a formal request for information that may be narrower, while a warrant is an order issued by a judge that may require production of specified data; Nextdoor's policy explains it responds to subpoenas, warrants, and certain emergency requests according to applicable law Nextdoor Privacy Policy

The transparency report summarizes requests but does not always show how local legal standards affect a specific request, so outcomes can vary by jurisdiction and by the exact nature of the legal order Nextdoor Transparency Report 2024

If you need case specific advice about a preservation or emergency disclosure request, consult an attorney rather than relying solely on general platform statements, since legal standards differ across jurisdictions and situations EFF guidance

If police contact you or request your posts: steps to take and what to say

First ask whether the request is accompanied by formal legal process and ask to see written documentation such as a subpoena or warrant before providing account details; civil liberties guidance recommends verifying the identity of the requester and requesting proof of legal authority EFF guidance and consider contacting counsel or contacting us if you need assistance.

Sample wording for informal requests that lack legal process includes brief refusals such as I cannot provide account information without written legal process, and Please provide a copy of any warrant or subpoena, noting you will consult counsel, which both protects your position and signals the need for formal procedure Nextdoor Privacy Policy

Common mistakes users make about Nextdoor and police access

A common misunderstanding is assuming neighborhood-only posts are immune from legal process; Nextdoor's documentation states neighborhood posts are available to verified neighbors and moderators but can be disclosed under lawful process Nextdoor Privacy Policy

Another frequent error is oversharing profile details such as full address or identifying numbers in posts or profile fields; the privacy settings help pages explain which profile fields are visible and how to limit them Nextdoor Help Center

Before posting, double check audience settings and membership boundaries for your neighborhood so you understand who can see a given post and reduce the chance you share more than intended Nextdoor Help Center
