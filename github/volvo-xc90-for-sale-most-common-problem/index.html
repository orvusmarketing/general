How to spot and evaluate XC90 air suspension issues before you buy

Confirm whether a listed vehicle actually has air suspension by checking the equipment list on the listing, the trim level, and the VIN-based options where available. If a listing or the VIN indicates air suspension, prioritize an inspection because air springs and compressors are the parts most often reported to fail on equipped XC90s RepairPal Volvo XC90 problems and costs.

On-vehicle checks are straightforward. Visually compare ride height side to side and corner to corner while the car is on level ground. Look for uneven sagging or a noticeably low corner after the vehicle has been parked for a few hours; that is a common sign of a leaking air spring CarComplaints XC90 suspension complaints.

During a short test drive, listen for frequent or noisy compressor cycles, especially right after start-up or when the vehicle should be at cruising ride height. A healthy system may run the compressor briefly, but repeated or prolonged cycles can indicate leaks or failing compressors or valves RepairPal repair and cost notes.

Inspect under the vehicle and around each strut for signs of oil, cracked air bellows or visible damage. If available, request service invoices that show prior compressor or strut replacement; parts and labor for compressors or air struts commonly range from several hundred to over a thousand dollars per component, depending on whether the compressor, air spring or associated valve block needs replacement RepairPal cost guidance.

What to check on electrified XC90s (T8 and hybrids) when considering a used listing

For T8 plug-in hybrids and other electrified XC90s, always run a VIN recall and service campaign check as a priority. High-voltage systems, charging components and regenerative braking systems have generated safety notices and service campaigns that are important to verify before purchase NHTSA recall and investigation records.

On a test drive and inspection, check the charging port for corrosion, damage or loose fittings, and confirm the charging cable and outlet interaction if the seller can demonstrate charging. Note any dashboard warnings that reference the high-voltage system or charging faults; these are signal events that should be cross-checked with service history Volvo Cars recall and service campaign information.

Evaluate regenerative braking behavior: it should engage smoothly and predictably according to pedal input and drive modes. Erratic regenerative braking feel, unexpected messages about the hybrid system, or inconsistent state of charge reporting are items to flag and to request service documentation for NHTSA records on electrified variants.

Long-term PHEV battery degradation for late-model XC90s is still an area with incomplete multi-year field data, so buyers should review documented battery checks, charging history and any battery-related warranty or service work when available Volvo Cars owner resources.

Electronic and infotainment faults: common symptoms and test steps

Owners and dealer records frequently report faults with the Sensus center display, intermittent warning lights and sensor errors on second-generation XC90s. These electronic issues often show as frozen or blank screens, random alerts, and intermittent failure of cameras or parking sensors Edmunds XC90 review and problems.

Run a short diagnostic checklist during a test drive. Cycle the center display through navigation, audio and vehicle menus, pair a phone over Bluetooth, and test voice commands if present. Check that cameras, parking sensors and blind-spot indicators activate and report correctly while driving slowly past a garage wall or in a parking lot RepairPal electronics and infotainment notes.

Watch for intermittent warning lights during ignition, startup and while driving. Small or sporadic errors may be inexpensive to fix, but persistent or multiple unrelated warnings can require deeper diagnostic time and dealer level attention, which increases repair cost and time Edmunds on electrical issues.

Transmission and powertrain issues: what varies by model year

Transmission and powertrain complaints are reported more frequently for first-generation XC90s (pre-2015), though later models show fewer but still present complaints in public databases. This pattern suggests model year matters when prioritizing gearbox checks on a used listing CarComplaints transmission reports.

On a test drive, watch for delayed engagement, hard shifts, shuddering under load and any persistent transmission warning light. These signs should prompt a careful review of service records for prior transmission service, fluid changes or documented repairs Consumer Reports guidance on drivetrain checks.

Ask for maintenance invoices that show scheduled transmission service and any clutch or gearbox repairs, and verify the presence of recent fluid changes when applicable. Older vehicles with incomplete transmission service history are higher risk and may justify a specialist inspection or price adjustment CarComplaints and service history notes.

Pre purchase inspection checklist for XC90 shoppers

Begin with a VIN lookup and recall check before you contact the seller, especially for electrified models and known safety campaigns. VIN checks help confirm whether recalls or service campaigns apply and whether previous repairs were recorded against the vehicle NHTSA VIN and recall lookup or Volvo Recall Information.

Prioritized inspection steps: 1) Confirm whether the vehicle has air suspension and run the ride height and compressor checks described earlier. 2) Test infotainment, displays and driver-assist sensors for intermittent behavior. 3) Review title status, ownership history and service invoices for documented work on suspension, electrified systems or transmission Volvo Cars owner resources on service campaigns.

Request specific documents: service invoices for air suspension repairs, any battery or charging repairs for T8 models, and recent diagnostic reports for electronic faults.
