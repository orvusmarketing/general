How the Grand Cherokee changed across generations and why that matters

The Jeep Grand Cherokee moved from the WK2 generation into a substantially revised WL design for 2022, and that redesign altered powertrains, electronics, and safety equipment. Treat pre 2022 and 2022 plus vehicles as different evaluation problems because parts, systems, and ratings are not directly transferable Edmunds expert reviews.

Earlier WK2 years show a pattern of owner complaints that improved in some refreshed later WK2 model years, while the WL redesign introduced new hardware and software that changed how safety features and drivetrains behave. For that reason, check crash test and recall records for the exact model year and trim you are considering rather than assuming a single reliability profile applies across generations IIHS vehicle ratings. When comparing parts availability and likely repair paths, remember a repair or service procedure for a pre 2022 model may not apply to a post 2022 vehicle because of different engines and control modules. That makes model year specificity essential for both diagnostic planning and cost estimates.

What to look for in a vehicle history report for a Grand Cherokee

Key fields to review are title status, ownership history, accident history, service records, and mileage checks. A clean title and single owner history lower ownership risk, and consistent service entries help explain major maintenance events RepairPal reliability data.

Red flags to treat cautiously include salvage or rebuilt title designations, inconsistent mileage entries, reported structural repair, and many short owner periods. Any of these can materially change expected ownership costs and should prompt deeper inspection and questions for the dealer NHTSA recall and complaint records.

Document differences between the report and the seller statements. If service history is missing for major intervals, ask why and seek receipts or independent shop records before you proceed to a hands on inspection.

Pre-purchase inspection checklist: what an independent mechanic should focus on

Ask the mechanic to inspect air suspension hardware if the vehicle has Quadra-Lift or adaptive air springs, because failures in this system are a commonly reported issue and can be costly to repair CarComplaints owner summaries.

Test transmission behavior under load and during highway merging. Transmission concerns appear in owner complaint datasets and may show as rough shifts, delayed engagement, or slipping under stress. Have the mechanic drive the vehicle and check for diagnostic codes if possible RepairPal reliability data. Also see KBB consumer reviews KBB.

Confirm all electrical accessories function, including infotainment, window motors, and interior modules, because electrical faults have been recurring across model years. Ask the mechanic to test lights, power seats, HVAC controls, and to scan for electronic fault codes CarComplaints owner summaries.

Inspect the frame and body for signs of structural repair, uneven panel gaps, or fresh paint in specific areas. Structural issues change safety and repair cost expectations and should be documented in the inspection report.

Common buying mistakes and how to avoid them

Skipping a vehicle history report or relying on seller statements alone exposes buyers to title and ownership risks. Always obtain a vehicle history report before visiting the dealer and compare its entries to the listing description RepairPal reliability data.

Assuming generational consistency is another common mistake.
