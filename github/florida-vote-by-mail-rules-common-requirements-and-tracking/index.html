Florida vote by mail rules: Quick overview and who can use it

Vote by mail, often called absentee voting, lets a registered Florida voter receive and return a ballot before Election Day. The Florida Division of Elections is the primary state authority that explains the process and provides links to county supervisors for local details Florida Division of Elections vote-by-mail page

Florida vote by mail rules: How to request a vote-by-mail ballot in Florida

You can request a ballot through the state portal or by applying directly with your county supervisor, using an online form or a paper application depending on the county. The Division of Elections portal links to county pages and explains the general steps to request an absentee ballot Florida Division of Elections vote-by-mail page

Many counties provide both a one-time request and a permanent status option. A one-time request applies only to the named election, while permanent status has the county mail ballots for all qualifying elections until you cancel or your registration changes; check your county supervisor site for the exact form and how to indicate permanent status Miami-Dade County vote by mail information

To complete a paper application, follow the form instructions exactly, including any identification fields and signature lines. If you request online, save or print confirmation and note any deadlines shown by the county. If a deadline is not clear on the state page, your county supervisor can confirm the last day to apply for a specific election.

Florida vote by mail rules: Deadlines and receipt requirements

Florida law requires that a voted absentee ballot be received by the supervisor of elections by the statutory deadline, commonly by 7:00 p.m. on Election Day, for the ballot to be counted. For the statutory framework and exact language, refer to Chapter 101 of the Florida statutes Chapter 101 - Elections (absentee ballots and related procedures)

Counties publish their specific calendars for application and mailing cutoffs, and those local schedules show recommended mailing windows to avoid late delivery. A common cause of ballots not being counted is late arrival, so confirm the county calendar and plan mailing with adequate lead time Miami-Dade County vote by mail information

If you request a ballot close to a deadline, consider faster delivery options or local hand delivery methods if your county permits them. The county calendar will list receipt cutoffs and any drop box locations the supervisor maintains.

When in doubt about a deadline, call the supervisor of elections to verify the cutoff and to ask about options if your ballot is in transit near Election Day. Supervisors can explain whether a replacement ballot is possible and the steps you should take.

Florida vote by mail rules: Signature requirements and verification

Florida law and Division of Elections guidance require that a voter sign the ballot return envelope and allow supervisors to reject ballots when the signature is missing or does not match the registration record. The outer certificate envelope is the official place for the voter signature and related information Florida Division of Elections vote-by-mail page

Supervisors compare the signature on the envelope to the voter registration record and may follow local procedures for signature review; when a signature is missing or does not match, the ballot can be rejected under current guidance Chapter 101 - Elections (absentee ballots and related procedures) (see Collier County Voter Signature FAQ for an example of local guidance).

To reduce the risk of a signature mismatch, sign exactly as your registration shows and avoid changing the signature style near the election. If your county offers a cure or correction process, follow the supervisor's instructions promptly to address a rejected signature.

Common reasons absentee ballots are rejected and how to avoid them

Missing or unsigned certificate envelopes are a frequent technical error that leads to rejection, so carefully follow the outer envelope instructions and confirm you signed in the correct place before sending your ballot Absentee voting in Florida overview

Other typical causes include signatures that do not match the voter file and ballots that arrive after the county receipt deadline. When a signature mismatch or late receipt occurs, the ballot can be rejected under current procedures Florida Division of Elections vote-by-mail page

To prevent these errors, verify your registration signature if you think it may have changed, follow the envelope directions exactly, and send your ballot with recommended lead time. If a rejection notice arrives, follow county instructions about whether a cure process or replacement is available.

Step-by-step checklist: Requesting, returning, and tracking your ballot

1. Confirm your voter registration and mailing address with the supervisor of elections before you request a ballot. Accurate registration information helps ensure the ballot is issued correctly Florida Division of Elections vote-by-mail page

2. Request the ballot through the state portal or your county supervisor's form. Note whether you are requesting a single ballot or permanent vote-by-mail status and save any confirmation you receive from the state or county Miami-Dade County vote by mail information (see our contact page for campaign inquiries).

3. When the ballot arrives, read all instructions, complete your choices, and place the ballot in the certificate envelope. Sign the outer envelope exactly as your registration shows and include any required identifying information.

Confirm registration and address, request the ballot early, sign the certificate envelope as registered, send or return the ballot with recommended lead time, and use county and USPS tracking to confirm receipt.

4. Seal the envelope carefully and send the ballot with recommended mailing lead time. If your county provides drop boxes or an in-person return option, consider those secure alternatives if they are convenient and permitted.

5. Use the state Track Your Ballot gateway and your county tracker to monitor issuance and receipt. If you also use USPS tracking for mailed ballots, compare both sources to confirm transit and delivery.

6. If your ballot is not received or shows as rejected, contact your county supervisor promptly to learn about replacement options or any cure process and to confirm what information you should provide.

What to do if your ballot is missing, rejected, or delayed

If a ballot is missing, first check the county tracker and any confirmation messages you received after requesting the ballot. County portals often show whether a ballot was issued or received and include office contact information for follow up Track Your Ballot state overview

If a ballot is rejected for signature or envelope issues, follow the notification instructions from the supervisor and act quickly. Some counties provide a cure process or instructions for submitting required corrections; contact the office immediately to learn the timeline and required documents Miami-Dade County vote by mail information

When a ballot is delayed in transit, compare USPS updates with the county tracker. If a replacement is needed, the supervisor can explain whether a replacement ballot or other remedy is available and the deadlines for obtaining it.

Permanent vote-by-mail status and how to apply

Permanent vote-by-mail status means a county will mail ballots to a registered voter for each election automatically until the voter cancels the status or the registration changes. This differs from a single-request ballot which applies only to one election Florida Division of Elections vote-by-mail page

To sign up for permanent status, use the Division of Elections portal or the county supervisor's designated form. Each county posts the form and instructions for applying or canceling permanent status on its vote-by-mail page Miami-Dade County vote by mail information

Before enrolling, confirm whether permanent status fits your needs, for example if you move or change registration you will need to update or cancel the status to avoid misdirected ballots. Contact your county supervisor to confirm how they manage permanent lists and how to cancel if you no longer want mailed ballots.

Technical tips for timely delivery and secure return

Send ballots with recommended lead time and follow your county's suggested mailing windows to minimize the chance of late arrival. County supervisors often publish suggested mailing dates for different types of elections and postal conditions Miami-Dade County vote by mail information

Consider secure return options such as official drop boxes or in-person delivery where your county allows them. Hand delivery to the supervisor's office or a designated drop box avoids postal transit variables and can provide quicker confirmation of receipt.

Always seal the certificate envelope exactly as instructed and double check that you signed in the correct place. Small packaging mistakes or omissions are common reasons ballots are rejected, so follow the envelope directions carefully.
