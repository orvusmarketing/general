What is Florida’s 25th Congressional District? Legal boundaries and map basis

Florida’s 25th Congressional District takes its legal shape from the state legislative redistricting plan approved in 2022 and applied for the 2024 through 2026 cycles, so the district lines in use today trace to that process, not to earlier maps Florida Redistricting Portal.

The current district includes portions of three South Florida counties and links metropolitan communities across a broad coastal and near-coastal area. For a clear view of where the district runs and how it intersects county borders, consult the official map products available through the state portal and nonpartisan summaries such as GovTrack.

Communities and geography in Florida’s 25th Congressional District

The district spans parts of the Miami metropolitan area, the Broward metropolitan area, and the Palm Beach metropolitan area, and that regional spread shapes daily life, commuting patterns, and local services inside the district, according to public district summaries Ballotpedia page for FL-25.

Practical scenarios: registering, requesting a mail ballot, and checking candidates in FL-25

Step-by-step: new voter registration

1. Confirm eligibility and prepare required information such as name, date of birth, and residence address, then access the state registration form or county portal.

2. Submit the registration by the state deadline for the election you want to vote in, and print or save the confirmation if the county system provides one. For exact steps and forms, consult the Division of Elections guidance Division of Elections.

Step-by-step: request and track a vote-by-mail ballot

1. Submit a vote-by-mail request through your county supervisor or use the state guidance to understand eligibility and timing.

2. After you receive the mailed ballot, follow the instructions precisely, sign where required, and return the ballot using the county-approved methods before the deadline.
