How to read service history and title status

Service records that show regular oil and filter changes, scheduled inspections and documented repairs for known issues reduce risk. Look for entries that align with the manufacturer recommended service schedule and that note completed recall repairs NHTSA recalls database.

Title flags to watch include salvage, rebuilt and flood. These statuses affect insurability and resale and require additional scrutiny; a rebuilt or salvage title often means higher long-term ownership risk and should prompt a careful inspection NHTSA recalls database.

Reconcile mileage entries across service records with the VIN mileage check in your vehicle history report. Discrepancies warrant further questions and may indicate odometer issues or record-keeping gaps NHTSA recalls database.

Common inspection findings and red flags to avoid

Inspectors often find electrical or infotainment faults, intermittent warning lights and evidence of water intrusion in some used XC90s. These issues can be time consuming and costly to diagnose and repair, so treat them as significant red flags RepairPal XC90 reliability.

Undisclosed collision damage and frame concerns are other frequent issues. Look for inconsistent panel gaps, mismatched paint and suspension or steering irregularities during the test drive. If you suspect undisclosed damage, insist on a structural inspection NHTSA recalls database.
