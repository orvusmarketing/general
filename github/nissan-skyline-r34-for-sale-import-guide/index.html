Each track has its own age thresholds and documentation expectations, so a car may meet one agency's criteria and not another. The NHTSA 25-year rule and what it means for R34 GT-R model years

NHTSA applies a 25-year rule that generally exempts vehicles 25 years old or older from meeting current Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards, which makes import without conversion possible in many cases. That exemption is based on model year and production date rather than on a seller's statement, so VIN-level verification is important. The agency describes the general exemption process and how it applies to older vehicles on its importing guidance page NHTSA importing guidance.

A GT-R is not legally importable to the US if it fails to meet applicable NHTSA safety standards or EPA emissions rules and cannot be brought into compliance, or if CBP denies entry due to missing or problematic documentation; eligibility often hinges on model year, production date and VIN.

For the R34 GT-R specifically, the commonly cited model years 1999 through 2002 become eligible under the 25-year rule in calendar years 2024 through 2027 respectively. However, exact eligibility depends on production dates tied to the VIN, so buyers should confirm the vehicle's build date and VIN details against NHTSA resources before assuming eligibility. EPA emissions rules and the 21-year exemption: an extra age threshold

The EPA enforces emissions requirements for imported vehicles but typically exempts vehicles that are at least 21 years old from emissions certification, creating a separate age threshold buyers must consider. Because the EPA threshold can come earlier than NHTSA's 25-year rule, a car can be exempt from emissions yet still require FMVSS compliance if it is under 25 model years. For details on which vehicles the EPA covers and its age-based exemptions consult the EPA emissions guidance EPA emissions guidance.

That mismatch between agency thresholds means buyers must verify both the EPA and NHTSA positions for a specific VIN and engine configuration, since emissions classification can depend on engine type and model year. When in doubt, contact EPA or use a customs broker to confirm the emissions status that will apply at the port of entry CBP vehicle entry guidance.

Importing R34 GT-Rs younger than 25 years: Registered Importers and Show-or-Display

If an R34 GT-R is younger than 25 years, the two primary compliance paths are conversion to FMVSS and EPA compliance through an NHTSA Registered Importer or applying for a limited Show-or-Display exemption for qualifying collector vehicles. Each path has limits: conversion requires an RI willing to accept the VIN and to certify modifications, while Show-or-Display is discretionary and carries use restrictions. For information about Registered Importers and the conversion process see the NHTSA Registered Importers page NHTSA Registered Importers page.

Customs, paperwork and duties: what CBP requires at entry

US Customs and Border Protection requires proper entry documentation, proof of ownership or title and payment of any applicable duties at the port of entry, and CBP enforces that NHTSA and EPA requirements are met before release. That means missing or inconsistent title paperwork, unresolved liens, or incomplete agency declarations can delay or block entry. For a concise outline of CBP expectations consult the CBP vehicle entry guidance CBP vehicle entry guidance.

Before initiating shipping, confirm the title status and lien history and obtain a written estimate for duties and port fees from your customs broker or carrier. If paperwork will arrive late, budget for storage and potential demurrage charges. Confirming clear title and documented ownership early reduces the chance that CBP will hold the vehicle at arrival CBP vehicle entry guidance.
