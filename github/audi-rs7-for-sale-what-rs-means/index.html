How the RS badge developed: a brief history and role of Audi Sport

Origins of the RS name

The RS designation traces to RennSport, a term Audi has used to connect select road cars to the company's motorsport work. Over time, RS became the shorthand that buyers and journalists use to mark Audi models with a stronger performance focus than standard A series or S models Audi RS models overview and for a broader historical overview see Audi S and RS models

Audi Sport GmbH and motorsport links

Audi Sport GmbH is the business unit that develops RS cars, drawing on motorsport expertise and engineering processes to adapt track technologies for road use. That engineering role explains why RS cars often receive bespoke components and more extensive mechanical upgrades than S line variants Audi Sport GmbH technical overview

How RS fits vs S and A models

Think of A models as the standard luxury or commuter offering, S models as a sportier intermediate step, and RS models as the most performance oriented road cars with engineering derived from motorsport practice. The intent differs: S models often tune software and trim for sportier feel while RS models change core hardware and calibration to reach higher sustained performance Audi RS models - Audi Sport

Pre purchase inspection checklist: RS7 focus items

Engine and turbo systems

Check for oil leaks, turbo shaft play, unusual noises and consistent boost delivery. Ask a mechanic to verify whether turbos and intercoolers match factory specifications and to look for signs of overheating or neglect

Drivetrain, differential and axles

Inspect the differential and driveshafts for play or noise and confirm that reinforced drivetrain components for RS models are present when listed. Verify service records for differential fluid changes and any gearbox work

Brakes, suspension and wheels

Examine rotor condition for scoring, measure remaining pad thickness and check suspension mounts for unusual wear. Also look for uneven tire wear and wheel offsets that suggest non factory setups
