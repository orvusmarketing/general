Quick answer: can an undocumented immigrant get a Florida driver license?

Short answer: under current Florida law and Florida Department of Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles policy, driver licenses and state identification cards require proof of lawful presence and are not issued to people who are not lawfully present in the United States, so undocumented immigrants are not eligible for a standard Florida license as of the cited agency guidance and statute. That combination is what creates the eligibility barrier for people without lawful presence. Florida Statute §322.03

What Florida law and DHSMV rules require to get a license

Statutory basis: Florida Statute §322.03

The statute describes application requirements and gives the state the authority to verify eligibility, including lawful presence, before issuing a license. That statutory framework is the primary source for eligibility rules applied across Florida. Florida Statute §322.03

DHSMV documentation list and lawful-presence requirement (legal immigration florida)

The DHSMV publishes a list of required documents applicants must present to get a license, such as proof of identity, proof of lawful presence, proof of Social Security number or an approved proof of ineligibility, and two proofs of Florida residency; the agency applies those document checks when processing applications. How Social Security number rules apply

DHSMV requires a Social Security number for applicants who are eligible for one and asks for proof of ineligibility when an applicant cannot provide an SSN; that requirement is part of the agency's documentation process and can be decisive for eligibility determinations. FLHSMV requirements page

Taken together, the statute and agency guidance mean that local driver license offices must follow the statewide rules on lawful presence and identity verification rather than make separate eligibility decisions. Florida Statute §322.03

REAL ID and federal rules: what they do and do not change

What REAL ID requires

REAL ID focuses on what documents a state needs to collect to issue a federally compliant credential, including proof of identity and lawful presence for those who seek a REAL ID-compliant card. DHS REAL ID overview

Why REAL ID does not create eligibility exceptions

Federal REAL ID guidance does not change a state's decision to require lawful presence; if a state requires lawful presence for any license, REAL ID rules do not authorize issuing a compliant card to someone the state deems ineligible. DHS REAL ID overview

How REAL ID fits with state documentation rules

Applicants seeking a REAL ID-compliant license should check the DHSMV documentation list, because state-required proof of lawful presence and identity remain necessary to satisfy both state and federal requirements. FLHSMV requirements page

Common misconceptions and mistakes to avoid

Myth: REAL ID allows undocumented residents to get a Florida license. In fact, REAL ID guidance does not create exceptions to a state's lawful-presence requirements. DHS REAL ID overview

Do not assume a county office can override state statute; county driver license offices apply DHSMV and statutory rules and generally cannot make exceptions for lawful-presence requirements. FLHSMV requirements page

Common application errors include missing primary identity documents, missing proof of residency, or not providing Social Security documentation when applicable; those errors are frequent causes of delays or denials. FLHSMV requirements page
