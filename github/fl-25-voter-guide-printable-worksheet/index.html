Quick start: What this FL-25 voter guide worksheet is and how to use it

One-page checklist overview - FL-25 voter guide

This one-page printable worksheet is a compact research tool designed to collect verifiable facts about FL-25 candidates for side-by-side comparison and for use at meetings or while you study the ballot.

Use the sheet to gather primary-source facts about filing status, committee finance totals, and direct candidate statements in one place.

Each numeric field on the worksheet should point to a primary source and include the date it was last checked to avoid sharing outdated figures.

Many voters prefer short, printable summaries and side-by-side tables for quick comparison, and research supports using concise formats with clear evidence links for trust and repeat consultation Pew Research Center summary.

Why use primary sources for an FL-25 voter guide

Authoritative records: what the FEC provides

For any finance or committee fields, consult the FEC as the authoritative record for candidate committee filings and itemized contributions, and cite the specific candidate or committee page next to the number you record FEC campaign finance data.

State verification: Florida Division of Elections and ballot status

To confirm who is qualified for Florida's 25th District ballot and to verify filing dates, use the Florida Division of Elections candidate lists and filing records; these state pages are the definitive place to check ballot status Florida Division of Elections candidate lists.

Neutral aggregator sites can help consolidate links and dates, but always cross-check a consolidated summary with the primary FEC and state filings.

Core worksheet framework: fields, sections, and a printable layout

Essential header fields (candidate name, party, filing status)

At the top of the sheet include candidate name, party, office sought, filing date, and ballot status; add a short biographical line and the source for that line.

For biography and status fields, list the campaign site or Ballotpedia as the quick reference and add the primary record where available Ballotpedia candidate page.

Campaign finance section: required fields and how to cite totals

Minimum finance fields for the worksheet

Required finance fields should include: committee name, total receipts, total disbursements, cash on hand, date of last filing, and the FEC candidate or committee page link for verification.

Use the FEC page when filling finance fields

How to link to primary finance records

Place the FEC page URL in the sources column and, where space is limited, include a short URL or QR code that points to the candidate or committee filing.

How to present donor context on a one-page sheet

Always pair any OpenSecrets donor breakdown with a link to the underlying FEC report so readers can confirm the raw data themselves FEC campaign finance data.

Neutral question bank: templated questions for a printable candidate questionnaire

Sample neutral questions recommended by voter guide best practice

Use consistently worded, neutral questions that avoid leading phrasing and that allow candidates to state priorities in their own words.

Examples: What are your top three policy priorities for this district? Please list any public offices you have held and dates. Describe any current professional roles and relevant experience. What are the names of your principal campaign committees and the date of the last FEC filing?

Start with primary records: use the FEC for finance totals, the Florida Division of Elections for filing and ballot status, and Ballotpedia or a campaign site only as supporting references; always cite the primary page and record the date you checked it.

Use these sample questions for candidate outreach or side-by-side print comparisons to keep answers comparable and easy to cite.

Follow League of Women Voters guidance on neutral wording and transparent sourcing when adapting question phrasing League of Women Voters guidance.

How to format answers and sources for printing

Ask candidates to provide short, one-line answers and to attach a URL for verification; place the source URL next to each answer on the sheet.

Date each answer when it was last verified so users can see which responses need rechecking.

Designing the one-page comparison table and printable layout

Column choices and readable fonts for print

Keep the layout to two or three candidate columns plus one narrow sources column; use a font size that remains legible when printed at standard letter or A4 size.

Choose a sans-serif font for headings and a plain serif or sans-serif for body text to improve printed readability.

How to include source links and 'last checked' dates

If space is tight, use short URLs or a QR code for the FEC and state pages and include a separate column for 'last checked' dates for each numeric field.

Flag items that need periodic rechecking, such as late endorsements or late-filed finance records, and mark them in a notes cell for follow-up.

Common mistakes and pitfalls when creating a voter worksheet

Avoiding unsourced numbers and rounding errors

Do not record rounded or unsourced finance totals; always take the number from the FEC filing and note the filing date next to it FEC campaign finance data.

Mistakes in attribution and biased question wording

Attribute campaign positions to their source with language such as 'according to the campaign site' or 'Ballotpedia lists' and avoid framing policy statements as guaranteed outcomes.

Steer clear of persuasive language, endorsements, or comparative claims that are not grounded in documented records League of Women Voters guidance.
