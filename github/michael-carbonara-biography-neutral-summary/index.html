Step-by-step framework to write a neutral Michael Carbonara biography

Structure to follow

Start with a single sourced sentence that states candidacy and district, then follow with short paragraphs that separate biographical facts, campaign stated priorities, and filings or finance notes.

For example, open with a line naming the office sought and the election cycle and attribute it to Ballotpedia or the campaign site, then move to background details with clear source notes for each claim Ballotpedia page for Michael Carbonara.

How to attribute each claim

Use simple attribution templates such as according to his campaign site for platform language and public FEC records show for finance numbers. When a campaign provides a claim without independent records, use phrasing like stated by the campaign or the campaign site states to avoid presenting the claim as independently verified Federal Election Commission help page.

Keep sentences short and place a citation or source note in the same paragraph as the claim so editors and readers can see the basis for the statement at a glance.

Verifying education, employment, and business claims

Primary documents to request

To verify education and employment, request degree verification from the issuing institution, corporate filings from the relevant state portal, and any professional licenses or registrations that apply to the subject.

If a campaign lists business titles or ownership, check secretary of state corporate records or public business registries and note the filing or registration dates rather than relying solely on the campaign About page Michael Carbonara - Official Campaign Website.

How to label unverified statements

If you cannot find an independent record, label the sentence reported by the campaign or stated by the campaign and provide the specific campaign page or press release date where the claim appears. This preserves accuracy while signaling remaining verification work Poynter Institute guidance on verification.

Avoid presenting unverified employment or business roles as established facts; instead say the campaign lists or the campaign states when referring to those claims.

How to use FEC and state filings for finance and filing details

What FEC records show

The FEC provides committee registration, periodic finance reports, and instructions for locating filings; use the FEC documents to report exact filing dates and the amounts listed on a given report rather than rounding or estimating FEC candidate overview.

State election offices can confirm candidate registration and whether a candidate met state filing requirements for the primary or general ballot.

How to cite filing dates and totals

When citing fundraising totals quote the figure exactly as shown on the report and include the report or filing date in the same sentence, for example public FEC records show $X reported on MM/DD/YYYY. Use the FEC report as the citation for that paragraph.

Do not approximate or round unless the source itself presents rounded figures; if you must summarize several reports, note the date range and reference the exact reports in editorial notes.

Deciding what to include: editorial criteria for neutral candidate bios

Relevance and verifiability

Include facts that can be confirmed in primary records or reputable reporting. Exclude unverified or promotional claims that cannot be tied to a primary or reputable secondary source Ballotpedia page for Michael Carbonara.

Prioritize official filings, campaign statements, and neutral profiles when choosing which details to publish, and keep platform language clearly attributed.

Avoiding evaluative language

Avoid adjectives that assess quality or outcome and avoid promises. Attribute policy language to the campaign with phrases such as according to his campaign site or the campaign states to keep copy neutral AP Stylebook guidance.

When a campaign uses a slogan, treat it as a quoted or attributed phrase rather than a statement of fact.

Common errors and how to avoid them

Misattribution and rounding

A common error is presenting campaign language without attribution; fix this by adding an explicit source note such as the campaign site states or according to Ballotpedia when the fact concerns candidacy status Michael Carbonara - Official Campaign Website.

Another frequent mistake is rounding finance totals. Avoid rounding by citing the exact figure with the report date from the FEC report you used as your source Federal Election Commission help page.

Using slogans as facts

Do not present slogans or branded phrases as objective outcomes. Instead place them in quotation marks and attribute them to the campaign or a campaign document.

If a slogan implies policy results, add attribution and a note that the claim is a campaign statement, not an independently verified outcome.

Attribution, quoting, and linking best practices

How to phrase attribution

Use short, consistent templates: according to his campaign site for statements of priority, Ballotpedia for candidacy status, and public FEC records show for finance numbers AP Stylebook guidance.

Place attribution near the claim and avoid piling multiple claims with a single source line; separate claims by sentence and attach the appropriate source to each sentence when possible.

When to quote verbatim

Quote verbatim when wording matters, such as for a slogan or a policy phrase. Always attribute the quote to the campaign and include the date of the source document.

Use links to primary documents in editorial notes for readers and editors, and include the report or publication date when quoting finance numbers or regulatory filings Federal Election Commission help page.

Handling conflicting information and gaps

How to report discrepancies

If two sources differ, present both claims with attribution and note which details lack independent confirmation. For example state that the campaign reports X while local reporting shows Y, and link each claim to its source in editorial notes Local reporting example and practices.

Do not synthesize conflicting claims into a single unqualified sentence; keep the attributions separate and clear.

Labeling gaps and next steps

When a detail cannot be independently verified, add language such as reported by the campaign and list follow up steps, for example requesting records or asking the campaign for documentation and noting the request date in your editorial log Poynter Institute guidance on verification.

Track outstanding verification tasks openly in editor notes to make pre publication checks straightforward.

Pre-publication checklist for editors

Verification steps

Confirm each factual claim has a cited primary or reputable secondary source and that any finance numbers include the exact FEC report date used as a source Federal Election Commission help page.

Style and legal checks

Run a final read for neutral tone, short sentences, and absence of evaluative adjectives or promises. Check that no typographic en or em dashes appear in the copy and that required links and notes are included for editors.

Confirm that the article follows applicable defamation and privacy checks for public figures and that direct quotes are attributed with a date and source.
